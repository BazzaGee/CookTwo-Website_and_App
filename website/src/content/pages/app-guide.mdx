## What CookTwo is

CookTwo is a shared food system for two people. One kitchen, two different goals. You and your partner (or just you, on your own) share a single grocery list, a single pantry, and a single AI meal planner — and the app figures out how to make dinner work for *both* of you from one pan.

If you've just downloaded it, here's the short version of how it's organised:

- **Shopping tab** — a real-time shared grocery list that syncs between both of you in under a second.
- **Pantry tab** — a typed list of what's in your kitchen, so the AI can suggest meals using food you already have.
- **Meals tab** — a chat with the AI that generates dinner ideas, splits the recipe into two plates with different macros for each of you, and adds anything you're missing to the shopping list.
- **Profiles tab** — your diet, your goals, your body profile, and the plan you're on (Free or Premium).

Everything is partner-coloured. **Partner A is sage green, Partner B is terracotta** — every item, every action, every notification has a small dot showing who added it.

That's the whole shape. Let's walk through every screen.

## Onboarding — setting up a shared kitchen

The first time you open CookTwo, you go through a short setup. There are 8-9 steps depending on whether you're cooking solo or with a partner, and you can skip the body profile and come back to it later.

### Step 1 — Welcome

The opening screen is the same whether you're cooking solo or with a partner. Three buttons at the bottom decide your path.

<GuideScreenshots
  src="/images/guide/guide-01-onboarding-welcome.webp"
  alt="CookTwo welcome screen with three onboarding options"
  caption="The welcome screen. Pick the path that matches your situation."
/>

- **Cooking solo** — sets up a one-person kitchen. You can invite a partner later from the Profiles tab.
- **I have a code** — for joining a kitchen your partner already started. You'll enter their 6-digit invite code on the next screen.
- **Setting up for two** — starts a brand-new household. You'll get an invite code at the end to share with your partner.

<GuideCallout>
**Picking the right path matters.** If your partner already has CookTwo, *don't* tap "Setting up for two" — you'll create a separate household and you'll never see each other's lists. Tap "I have a code" and use their 6-digit code instead.
</GuideCallout>

### Step 5 — Body profile (optional)

Height, weight, age, gender, and activity level. The app uses these numbers to calculate TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) — the actual number of calories your body burns in a day — and then subtracts (for weight loss) or adds (for muscle gain) to set your target.

- **Weight, height, age, gender** — for the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, a resting metabolic rate formula that's widely regarded as the most accurate for healthy adults.
- **Activity level** — affects how much your TDEE is. "Sedentary" is desk work, no exercise. "Very active" is hard daily training.

The shell has three parts:

- **The header (H)** — partner dots and names on the left, six icons on the right. Always there, never scrolls. The partner dots are the small coloured circles with initials: Partner A is sage green, Partner B is terracotta. If you're cooking solo, you'll only see one dot.
- **The main content** — what changes when you switch tabs. Scrolls vertically. On the Shopping tab, the add-item input sits at the bottom of the screen, just above the nav. On the Pantry and Meals tabs, content fills the screen from top to bottom — there's no fixed input bar at the bottom.
- **The bottom nav (N)** — three tabs: Shopping, Pantry, and Meals. These are the three primary daily-use tabs. Profiles & Settings live behind the gear icon in the header, not here. Each tab has a small icon and a label. The active tab has a coloured indicator.

The shell is consistent across every tab. Once you learn it, you never have to think about where things are again.

## The Shopping tab

The Shopping tab is where most of your day-to-day lives. It's a real-time, partner-coloured, AI-categorised grocery list.

<GuideScreenshots
  src="/images/guide/guide-11-shopping-populated.webp"
  alt="CookTwo shopping tab populated with categories"
  markers={[
    { label: "C", x: 96, y: 30, tx: 55, ty: 25 }
  ]}
  caption="The full populated list. Categories, partner dots, and item counts."
/>

Here's what you're seeing:

- **Title with item count** — the header shows how many items are left to buy.
- **Category cards (C)** — Produce, Meat, Dairy, Pantry, Household, Personal Care. Each category is a collapsible card. A single tag on the right (`2 to buy`, `1 to buy`, `done`) shows how many unchecked items are in that category. Tap the category header to collapse it. Inside each card, every item has:
  - A **checkbox** — tap to check off
  - **Quantity** in parens (e.g. "Chicken Breast 500g")
  - A **partner dot** on the right (sage for Partner A, terracotta for Partner B) — shows who added it
  - A small **×** on hover (or tap-and-hold on mobile) to delete
  - A **move** action — tap to re-file the item into a different category if it landed in the wrong one
- **Add input** — type more items. Commas separate them.

The AI sorts your items into categories automatically based on the item name. "Sourdough bread" goes to Pantry. "Chicken breast" goes to Meat. "Baby spinach" goes to Produce. If something ends up in the wrong category, the move action lets you fix it in one tap.

<GuideCallout>
**Pro tip — the partner dot means something.** When you tap an item to check it off, the *other* person sees the change within a second, and they see *who* checked it. The notification appears in their bell feed. This is the bit that ends the "did you add the milk?" text.
</GuideCallout>

### Checking items off and the "Done shopping" flow

As you walk through the supermarket, you tap each item to check it off. Checked items move to the bottom of their category, with a strikethrough.

<GuideScreenshots
  src="/images/guide/guide-12-shopping-done.webp"
  alt="CookTwo shopping tab with items checked off and the Done shopping button"
  caption="Items checked off, strikethrough applied. The Done shopping button is at the bottom of the list."
/>

When you've checked everything off and you're ready to put the groceries away, tap the big **Done shopping** button at the bottom. It's smart about what it does:

- Food items you checked off get **moved to your Pantry** automatically (so you don't have to type them in again).
- Non-food items (dish soap, toothpaste, etc.) just get removed from the list.

The button tells you exactly what's going to happen: "Done shopping · 2 food items → pantry · 1 non-food removed".

<GuideCallout>
**Done shopping and Generate Meal are the two most important buttons in the app.** Checking items off is one half. Moving them to the Pantry when you're home is the other half. And generating a meal from your pantry ties it all together. If you only ever tap two buttons in CookTwo, make them these.
</GuideCallout>

## The Pantry tab

The Pantry is your "what's in the kitchen right now" list. It feeds the AI meal planner — meals are built around what's already in your pantry before adding anything new.

<GuideScreenshots
  src="/images/guide/guide-14-pantry-populated.webp"

  caption="The Pantry tab with items. Categories are the same as the Shopping list — Produce, Meat, Dairy, Pantry, Household, Personal Care."

The Pantry uses the same category emojis as the Shopping list: 🥬 Produce, 🥩 Meat, 🥛 Dairy, 🫙 Pantry, 🏠 Household, 🧴 Personal Care. Each category is a collapsible card. Inside each card, items have:

- **Quantity** and **brand** in parens (e.g. "1L Whole Milk", "Swiss Cheddar (250g, Sainsbury's)")
- A **partner dot** on the right
- A small **×** to delete the item

The top-right of the list has `+ Expand all` / `− Collapse all`. Useful when you have a long list.

<GuideCallout>
**It pays to be detailed here too.** When you add an item, write the exact quantity and brand. **"400g T-Bone steak"** tells the AI exactly how much steak you have. **"5 whole grape fruits"** is unambiguous. **"250ml virgin olive oil"** tells the AI the bottle size. When the AI generates a meal, it uses these specifics to portion your plate. And after a meal is plated, it adjusts your pantry down to the gram — showing you exactly how much of each item you have left.
</GuideCallout>

## The Meals tab — Tonight and Saved

The Meals tab is the AI planner. It has two views in a pill switcher at the top: **Tonight** and **Saved**.

### Tonight — empty state

<GuideScreenshots
  src="/images/guide/guide-15-meals-empty.webp"
  alt="CookTwo meals tab Tonight empty state"
  caption="Tonight, empty. Tap the big 'Generate a meal' button to start."
/>

The first time you open the Meals tab, you see a single button: **Generate a meal from what's in your pantry**. This is the default — the AI looks at your pantry and suggests a meal you can cook tonight without going to the shop. Tap it.

You'll also see a second option:

- **Suggest a meal — I'll shop** — plans a meal based on what you'd like, and adds any missing ingredients to your shopping list.

The chat interface below the buttons is where the conversation happens. You can type follow-up questions like "what can I substitute for chicken?" or "make it vegetarian" — and the AI will adjust the suggestion. Every interaction in this chat counts as one AI request against your daily quota.

### Tonight — the meal card

After you pick a mode, the AI responds with a chat-style message and a meal card.

<GuideScreenshots
  src="/images/guide/guide-16-meals-tonight-card.webp"
  alt="CookTwo meal chat with generated meal card"
  caption="A generated meal in the chat. The card shows macros, what's in pantry, what you need to buy, and action buttons."
/>

The meal card has:

- **Name and description** — e.g. "Lemon Herb Chicken with Quinoa — bright, herby, ready in 30 min."
- **Time** — total time in minutes.
- **In pantry / To buy** — how many ingredients you already have, how many you need.
- **Macros** — four small stats: calories, protein, carbs, fat (per plate by default).
- **Action buttons**:
  - **Add X to list** — add the missing ingredients to your shopping list. Turns green and says "Added" after you tap.
  - **Save** — saves the recipe to your Saved list.

Tap **Show ingredients & steps** to expand the full recipe.

### Tonight — adaptive plating (the magic part)

This is the bit that makes CookTwo different from any other meal planner. When both partners have set their goals and body profiles, the AI sizes the same recipe into two different plates — one for each of you. Same pan, two portions.

<GuideScreenshots
  src="/images/guide/guide-17-meals-tonight-plating.webp"
  alt="CookTwo meal card expanded with adaptive plating"
  caption="Adaptive plating. Same pan, two plates, two different macro splits."
/>

There are two cards side by side. Each one shows:

- **Whose plate** — Partner A (sage border) or Partner B (terracotta border).
- **The portion** — exact amounts in real units (e.g. "6oz chicken, 1 cup quinoa, side of greens").
- **Macros** — protein, carbs, fat for that plate.
- **The total** — calories and the goal it serves (e.g. "~700 cal · Muscle gain").

The cooking steps are the same for both of you. You cook one pan. At the end, you plate it differently.

<GuideCallout>
**The plating is per-person, not per-recipe.** The recipe is the same. The macros are different because the portions are different. If you're both trying to maintain, the portions will end up roughly equal. If one of you is cutting, theirs will be smaller.
</GuideCallout>

### Saved recipes

<GuideScreenshots
  src="/images/guide/guide-18-meals-saved.webp"
  alt="CookTwo Meals tab Saved view"
  caption="The Saved view. Every meal you've saved, with status badges."
/>

When you save a meal from the Tonight card, it shows up in the Saved view. Saved recipes remember their ingredients, steps, and plating. The status badge on the right tells you whether you can cook it right now (in pantry) or need to shop first (X to buy).

Tap any saved recipe to expand it and see the full details again — ingredients, steps, and plating cards for both partners. You can also tap **Add X to list** right from the saved view to restock the ingredients you're missing.

Saved recipes are unlimited on both Free and Premium plans. There's no cap on how many you can save.

The second card on the Profiles tab is your partner's. By default, you can't edit it — the body profile is locked to the other partner unless they've explicitly opted in by switching on the "Visible to your partner" toggle in their own settings. Only the person whose profile it is can change their body stats, diet, and goals. The meal planner still uses both profiles to calculate plating — it just doesn't show one partner the other's numbers unless they've chosen to share.

You can see your partner's name, diet, and allergies on their card. Body stats are hidden by default. If they've toggled on visibility, you'll see their weight, height, age, gender, activity level, and calorie target. The calorie target updates in real time as they change their goal or body stats.

## AI credits and the free vs Premium tiers

The AI is metered. The whole app runs on a daily quota that resets at midnight in your local timezone. The quota is *shared* with your partner — you don't get two separate limits, you get one shared pool.

### The chip in the header

The pill badge in the header (the `10/10` thing) is your live usage. Tap it to open the full panel.

<GuideScreenshots
  src="/images/guide/guide-22-usage-panel.webp"
  alt="CookTwo AI credits usage panel"
  caption="The usage panel. A progress bar, a reset countdown, and an upgrade button on the Free plan."
/>

The panel shows:

- **AI requests** — `4/10` used today, with a progress bar that goes sage → amber → terracotta as you burn through your quota.
- **Resets in** — countdown to midnight in your timezone, when the quota resets.

The chip color changes as you get closer to your limit:

- **Sage** (0–49% used) — you're fine
- **Amber** (50–79% used) — getting close
- **Terracotta** (80–100% used) — almost out, the panel opens to the upgrade prompt

The quota is shared between you and your partner. If you generate a meal, it counts against the same pool. If your partner generates one, that counts too. You're working from a single daily budget.

### The two plans side by side

<GuideScreenshots
  src="/images/guide/guide-31-plan-table.webp"

The yearly plan saves 25% vs monthly. Cancel anytime from the **Manage subscription** button (Premium only) — it opens a Stripe portal where you can cancel, change cards, or download invoices.

<GuideCallout>
**What "10 AI requests" really means.** One request = one round-trip to the AI. Generating a single meal is 1 request. Asking "what can I substitute for chicken?" in the chat is 1 request. If you find yourself running out at 9pm, you're using more than you think.
</GuideCallout>

- Add items to the shopping list
- Add items to the pantry
- Check items off
- Browse everything

Anything you change is queued in local storage. The moment your connection comes back, everything syncs to your partner's phone. They see your offline additions as if you did them right then.

### Sign out

The exit icon on the far right of the header signs you out. You'll need to log back in with your name and (if you have one) your partner's invite code. There's no password — auth is by device for now.

If you want to permanently leave a household, sign out, then sign up again solo (or with a different code).

## Common questions

**Can I use CookTwo solo?**
Yes. Tap "Cooking solo" on the welcome screen. You get a household of one. Everything works the same — you just don't have a second partner colour.

**What if my partner and I have very different diets (e.g. vegan + omnivore)?**
The AI handles it. Each of you sets your own diet in the Profiles tab. The adaptive plating adjusts portions to fit both. Ingredients that don't fit one partner's diet show up as "missing" for them but not the other.

**Why are my macros different from my partner's?**
Same recipe, different portions. The AI sizes each plate to match that person's goal (lose / maintain / gain) and TDEE. You cook the same pan.

**Can I undo a delete?**
Not yet. Be careful with the × button. We have it on the roadmap.

**Where is my data stored?**
On Cloudflare's D1 database, associated with your household. Encrypted in transit and at rest. You can sign out and delete your data by emailing us.

**Does it work on desktop?**
Yes — the PWA runs in any modern browser. Pin it to your dock or taskbar for an app-like experience.

**Is there a dark mode?**
Not yet. Cream and sage are the brand. Dark mode is on the roadmap for late 2026.

**What about Apple Watch / Wear OS?**
Not yet. The PWA is mobile-first for now.

**How do I know if I'm on Free or Premium?**
Check the gear icon → Profiles tab. Your current plan is shown at the bottom. The usage chip in the header also reflects your plan — Free shows `10/10` and Premium shows `70/70` at the start of each day.

**What happens if I run out of AI requests?**
You can still browse, check off items, and manage your pantry. You just can't generate new meals or chat with the AI until the quota resets at midnight. Premium users get 7x more headroom.
