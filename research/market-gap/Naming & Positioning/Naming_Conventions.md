# Cupla — Naming Conventions & Name Viability Research

## Every name candidate, structured by pattern, with web-researched availability and competitor conflict analysis.

---

## 1. Name Pattern Structure

The naming convention follows a three-part formula:

**[Relationship Word] + [Domain Word] + [Category Noun]**

| Component | Purpose | Examples |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| Relationship Word | Signals who the app is for | Couples, Shared, Our, We, Duo, Pair, Together |
| Domain Word | Signals what space the app operates in | Food, Meal, Dinner, Kitchen, Plate, Pantry, Cook |
| Category Noun | Signals what kind of product it is | Planner, System, App, Hub, OS, Companion, Sync |

By mixing and matching across these three components, you get every possible name in this naming convention.

## 9. Strategic Recommendation

### The Naming Architecture

Rather than picking just one name, consider a naming architecture with three layers:

| Layer | Purpose | Recommended Name |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| **Brand name** | What appears on the app icon, App Store, marketing | **One Dinner** or **Two Plates** or **PairPlate** |
| **Positioning statement** | What appears under the brand name in all copy | "The Couples Food System" |
| **Marketing hook** | What appears in ads, social, and press | "Same meal. Two plates." |

### Example architectures:

**Architecture A:**
> **One Dinner** — The Couples Food System
> *"Same meal. Two plates."*

**Architecture B:**
> **Two Plates** — The Couples Food System
> *"Cook once. Plate twice."*

**Architecture C:**
> **PairPlate** — The Couples Food System
> *"One dinner. Two plates. Both perfect."*
