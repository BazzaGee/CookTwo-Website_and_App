---

## 1. Executive Summary

WhatDinner is a minimal meal planning app for couples that uses a swipe-based interface for deciding meals together. Available on iOS with an Android waitlist, it offers a recipe manager with frequency-based preferences and a simple planning interface. The app has an extremely lightweight website and appears to be a small indie project with limited ongoing development. Its swipe-to-decide mechanic is its most distinctive feature but the overall feature set is thin.

## 17. Key Takeaways & Recommendations for Cupla

1. **Concept worth borrowing.** The swipe-to-deide mechanic and frequency tracking are clever UI patterns. Cupla could incorporate a "swipe to pick" mode for meal selection, and track recipe frequency to avoid repetition automatically.

2. **Validates the decision problem.** WhatDinner exists purely to solve "what do we eat?" — confirming this is a real pain point worth addressing.

3. **Missing the entire pipeline.** WhatDinner stops at the decision. Cupla covers decide → plan → shop → cook. The depth difference is enormous.

4. **No grocery or pantry integration.** After deciding what to eat, users are on their own. Cupla's integrated grocery list and pantry awareness complete the workflow.

5. **Frequency tracking is a good idea.** Cupla should track how often recipes are cooked and factor that into AI suggestions to prevent "food fatigue."

6. **Lesson for Cupla:** Gamification elements like swiping can make meal planning more engaging, but they must be part of a complete system — not the entire product. Cupla can add playful decision mechanics without sacrificing depth.
