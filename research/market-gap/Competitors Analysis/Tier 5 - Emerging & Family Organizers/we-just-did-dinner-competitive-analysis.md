---

## 1. Executive Summary

We Just Did Dinner is a lightweight web-based meal planning tool targeting couples who want to plan dinners together. Built on the Lovable (AI website builder) platform, it offers recipe library management, drag-and-drop meal planning, and auto-generated grocery lists. The site is live and functional but shows signs of being an early-stage or hobby project with limited user acquisition, no mobile apps, and no apparent revenue model.

## 17. Key Takeaways & Recommendations for Cupla

1. **No competitive threat.** We Just Did Dinner is a minimal MVP with no users, no revenue, and no technical depth. It proves there is interest in the couples meal planning space but executes at a hobby-project level.

2. **Validated pain point.** The brand story ("we just did dinner!") mirrors the exact frustration Cupla addresses. This validates demand.

3. **Missing the sync layer entirely.** The biggest gap — it positions itself for couples but offers no actual collaborative features. Cupla's real-time sync is a significant differentiator.

4. **No pantry intelligence.** The product stops at meal planning. Cupla's pantry-first AI approach is a generation ahead.

5. **No offline/PWA.** Being web-only with no offline support means it fails in the two places meal planning matters most: the grocery store and the kitchen.

6. **Lesson for Cupla:** Even basic couples positioning resonates. Cupla should lean into the relationship-first narrative since no competitor currently delivers on that promise with substance.
