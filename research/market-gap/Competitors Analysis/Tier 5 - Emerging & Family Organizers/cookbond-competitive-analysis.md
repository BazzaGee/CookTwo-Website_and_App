**URL:** https://cookbond.co  
**Threat Level:** LOW  
**Analysis Date:** May 2026  
**Status:** ACTIVE (iOS app live, solo developer)

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Cookbond is an iOS-only cooking app designed specifically for couples. Its unique differentiator is the "Me / You / Us" role-assignment system that divides cooking steps between two partners. The app focuses on the collaborative cooking experience itself (the act of cooking together) rather than meal planning or grocery management. It is built by a solo developer (Salih Firat Simsek) and launched on the App Store in November 2025. With no ratings/reviews visible and limited features, it remains a niche early-stage product.

## 3. Core Features Analysis

### 3.1 Step-by-Step Recipes
- Detailed cooking instructions with clear steps
- Ingredients and timing included
- Curated "Date Night Packs" organized by mood/vibe

### 3.2 Me / You / Us Role System
- Assigns cooking tasks between partners
- "Me" = one partner's tasks, "You" = other partner's tasks
- "Us" = collaborative moments (taste, stir, plate together)
- Prevents elbow-bumping and role confusion

### 3.3 Photo Memories
- Capture and save photos of cooking adventures
- Build a personal cookbook of memories

### 3.4 Premium Recipes
- Chef-curated recipe collections
- Themed date night packs (e.g., "Spice Sizzle," "Roman Holiday," "Sunday Morning Snuggles")
- Individual packs available as IAP ($1.99 each)

### 3.5 Plans
- Schedule recipes for specific dates
- Reminder notifications
- Shopping list generation for planned recipes

## 12. SWOT Analysis

### Strengths
- Truly unique Me/You/Us role system
- Strong relationship-focused narrative

### Weaknesses

- No real-time sync between devices
- Solo developer dependency
- Narrow feature scope (cooking experience only)
- No pantry, planning, or grocery depth

### Opportunities
- Web version would dramatically expand reach
- Adding meal planning could create a full couples food system
- Role-based cooking could complement Cupla's planning features

### Threats
- Cupla could replicate the role concept as a cooking mode feature
- Any meal planning app adding "cook together" mode
- Solo developer sustainability risk

## 17. Key Takeaways & Recommendations for Cupla

1. **Concept worth studying.** Cookbond's Me/You/Us role system is a genuinely novel interaction pattern for couples cooking. Cupla should consider a "Cook Mode" feature that assigns tasks between partners.

2. **Relationship positioning is validated.** Cookbond's entire brand is built around cooking as a relationship experience — this validates Cupla's relationship-first UX thesis.

3. **Narrow scope is a ceiling.** Cookbond only owns the cooking moment. Cupla owns the entire pipeline (plan → shop → cook → eat). This gives Cupla a massive scope advantage.

4. **No real-time sync is fatal for couples.** Cookbond requires both partners to look at the same device. Cupla's real-time sync is a structural advantage.

5. **Solo developer risk.** A single developer maintaining a niche iOS app poses minimal competitive risk. No scaling potential.

6. **Lesson for Cupla:** The date night / experience angle is emotionally resonant. Cupla could incorporate "date night" themes or moods into its meal planning to add relationship warmth without sacrificing system depth.
