## What We Do That Others Don't

### 1. Adaptive Portions From One Meal

**Others:** Every meal planning app gives you ONE portion size. If you and your partner have different goals, you're expected to either eat the same portions (compromising your goals) or cook two completely separate meals (doubling your work).

**Us:** Our AI generates ONE shared cooking workflow with TWO distinct plating instructions. Same pan, same recipe, different plates. Partner A gets 450 calories optimized for weight loss. Partner B gets 700 calories optimized for muscle gain. You cook together and eat together.

### 2. Real-Time Collaboration Built In

**Others:** Most apps sync data periodically or require manual refreshing. Some popular grocery apps charge extra for real-time sync. Most diet trackers are entirely solo experiences.

**Us:** Changes appear on your partner's device in under 100 milliseconds. Add milk to the list from the dairy aisle — your partner sees it at home instantly. No refreshing, no waiting, no phone calls.

### 3. Pantry-First Intelligence

**Others:** Most apps start with recipes and generate a grocery list. They ignore what you already have in your kitchen. You end up buying ingredients you already own.

**Us:** We invert the model. Tell us what you have — "chicken, rice, spinach, half an onion" — and our AI generates meals using your existing ingredients. Less waste, fewer trips to the store, more money saved.

### 4. Relationship-First Design

**Others:** Most nutrition apps feel clinical, isolating, and fitness-obsessed. They're built for individuals tracking macros. The UX is cold, analytical, and single-user.

**Us:** Every interaction uses "we" language. The app feels like a shared digital kitchen, not a medical chart. We sell less stress and smoother evenings — the food is just the medium.

### 5. No App Store Required

**Others:** Download from the App Store. Create an account. Verify your email. Wait for your partner to do the same. High friction for second-partner adoption.

**Us:** Share a link. Your partner taps it. Works instantly in their browser. Install to home screen if they want. No app store, no download friction, no review process.

## Generic Competitor Types (For Compare Page)

### Type 1: "Popular meal planning apps"

- Built for one person
- Single calorie target, single portion size
- One grocery list for one user
- **Gap:** Useless for couples with different goals

### Type 2: "Standard diet tracking apps"

- Know your macros perfectly
- Don't know your partner exists
- Clinical, solo experience
- **Gap:** No collaboration, no shared kitchen concept

### Type 3: "Grocery list apps"

- Sync shopping lists brilliantly
- Zero nutritional intelligence
- No meal planning or AI
- **Gap:** Don't know what to cook or what your body needs

### Type 4: "AI meal generators"

- Generate creative recipes
- Plan for one person only
- Don't understand shared kitchens
- **Gap:** No multi-user, no adaptive portions, no real-time sync

### Type 5: "Couples-focused apps"

- Understand the relationship angle
- Usually lack AI and nutrition intelligence
- Often platform-limited (iOS only)
- **Gap:** Missing the technical depth and adaptive cooking
