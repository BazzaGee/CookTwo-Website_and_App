# Keto / Low-Carb Diet — Food Framework

> Practical guide to what to eat, avoid, and limit on the ketogenic diet, including macronutrient targets, portion guidelines, and sample meal structures.

---

## Macronutrient Targets

### Standard Ketogenic Diet (SKD)

| Macronutrient | Target (% of total calories) | Grams (on 2,000 cal diet) | Notes |
|---------------|:----------------------------:|:-------------------------:|-------|
| **Carbohydrates (net)** | 5–10% | **20–50g** | Net carbs = total carbs − fiber. Keep under threshold to maintain ketosis |
| **Protein** | 15–25% | 75–125g | Enough to maintain muscle mass; do NOT over-restrict protein |
| **Fat** | 70–80% | 155–178g | Primary energy source; mix of saturated and unsaturated |
| **Fiber** | Varies | 15–30g | From low-carb vegetables, nuts, seeds — track net carbs |
| **Sodium** | 3,000–5,000 mg | — | Higher than standard — keto increases sodium excretion; needs supplementation |
| **Potassium** | 3,500–4,700 mg | — | From avocado, leafy greens, meat, fish |
| **Magnesium** | 400–500 mg | — | From nuts, seeds, leafy greens; supplement often needed |

> **Net carbs:** Most keto practitioners track "net carbs" (total carbohydrates minus fiber and sugar alcohols), as fiber does not significantly impact blood glucose or insulin. The 20–50g net carb threshold is what most people need to maintain nutritional ketosis (blood BHB >0.5 mmol/L).

### Target by Diet Sub-Type

| Variant | Carbs (% cal) | Protein (% cal) | Fat (% cal) | Notes |
|---------|:-------------:|:---------------:|:-----------:|-------|
| **Classical Keto (4:1)** | ~2% | ~8% | ~90% | Therapeutic epilepsy; by weight ratio 4:1 fat:(protein+carb) |
| **Standard Keto (SKD)** | 5–10% | 15–25% | 70–80% | Most common adult version |
| **Modified Atkins (MAD)** | ~5% | ~30% | ~65% | Easier epilepsy treatment; no calorie restriction |
| **Targeted Keto (TKD)** | 5–10% + pre-workout carbs | 15–25% | 70–80% | Add 25–50g fast carbs 30–60 min before exercise |
| **Cyclical Keto (CKD)** | SKD 5 days + high-carb 2 days | Variable | Variable | For athletes/bodybuilders |

## Foods to Eat Freely (The Foundation)

### Fats and Oils (Primary Energy Source)

| Food | Type | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| **Extra virgin olive oil** | Monounsaturated | Best all-purpose; use for dressings and low-heat cooking |
| **Avocado oil** | Monounsaturated | High smoke point; best for cooking |
| **Butter** | Saturated | Grass-fed preferred (higher in CLA and vitamin K2) |
| **Ghee** | Saturated | Clarified; higher smoke point than butter |
| **Coconut oil** | Saturated | Contains MCTs; supports ketone production |
| **MCT oil** | Saturated (medium-chain) | Rapidly converts to ketones; popular supplement |
| **Tallow / lard / duck fat** | Saturated/Mono | Animal fats for cooking |

## Foods to Avoid Completely

| Food | Reason |
|------|--------|
| **All grains** | Wheat, rice, oats, corn, barley, rye, quinoa — all are high in carbs (50–75% carb by weight) |
| **Sugar (all forms)** | Table sugar, honey, maple syrup, agave, coconut sugar — all break ketosis |
| **Starchy vegetables** | Potatoes, sweet potatoes, yams, cassava, corn, winter squash |
| **Most fruit** | Apples, bananas, grapes, oranges, mangoes, pineapples — too high in fructose |
| **Legumes** | Lentils, chickpeas, beans (black, kidney, pinto) — high in carbs (~20-30g net/100g) |
| **Pasta, bread, cereal** | All wheat-based products are carb-dense |
| **Sugary drinks** | Soda, juice, sports drinks, sweetened coffee/tea |
| **Most condiments (sweetened)** | Ketchup, BBQ sauce, teriyaki, sweet chili — contain hidden sugar |
| **Beer** | 10–15g carbs per serving (some light beers acceptable in moderation) |
| **Low-fat/fat-free products** | Usually have added sugar to replace fat |
| **Processed "low-carb" junk food** | Keto-branded snacks with maltitol can spike blood sugar in some |

## Daily Eating Pattern (Sample Day — Standard Keto)

### Breakfast
- 3 eggs scrambled in butter with spinach and cheese
- 2 slices of bacon
- Half an avocado
- Coffee with 1 tbsp MCT oil or heavy cream
- *Or:* "Bulletproof" coffee (coffee blended with butter + MCT oil) for intermittent fasting practitioners

### Lunch
- Grilled chicken thigh salad: mixed greens, cucumber, avocado, olive oil dressing
- Handful of macadamia nuts
- *Or:* Tuna salad (with full-fat mayo, celery) over lettuce cups

### Snack (optional)
- Handful of almonds or walnuts
- String cheese or a few slices of cheese
- *Or:* Celery sticks with cream cheese

### Dinner
- Ribeye steak or salmon fillet (170–225g)
- Roasted cauliflower with butter
- Asparagus sautéed in olive oil
- Side salad with olive oil and vinegar

### Dessert (optional)
- Handful of raspberries with heavy whipped cream
- *Or:* 1–2 squares of 85%+ dark chocolate

> **Typical daily macros on this plan:** ~1,800–2,200 calories, ~20–35g net carbs, ~120–150g protein, ~150–180g fat

## Electrolyte Management (Critical During Adaptation)

The first 1–3 weeks of keto adaptation involve significant fluid and electrolyte loss as glycogen stores are depleted. **"Keto flu" symptoms** (headache, fatigue, nausea, dizziness, muscle cramps) are almost always due to electrolyte deficiency, not ketosis itself.

| Electrolyte | Daily Target | Sources |
|-------------|:------------:|---------|
| **Sodium** | 3,000–5,000 mg | Salt food liberally; bone broth; pickle juice; electrolyte supplements |
| **Potassium** | 3,500–4,700 mg | Avocado (975mg each!), leafy greens, salmon, mushrooms, meat |
| **Magnesium** | 400–500 mg | Pumpkin seeds, almonds, spinach; magnesium glycinate supplement |
| **Water** | 2–3+ liters | Drink to thirst; ketosis is diuretic |

> **Rule of thumb:** If you feel "keto flu" symptoms, drink a glass of water with ½ tsp salt + a squeeze of lemon. Symptoms usually resolve within 30 minutes.

## Ketone Monitoring Methods

| Method | What It Measures | Accuracy | Cost |
|--------|-----------------|:--------:|------|
| **Blood ketone meter** | Blood BHB (mmol/L) | ★★★★★ Gold standard | Moderate (test strips ~$1-3 each) |
| **Breath ketone analyzer** | Acetone in breath | ★★★☆☆ Good | High (device), low per-test |
| **Urine ketone strips** | Acetoacetate in urine | ★★☆☆☆ Fair (only useful early) | Very cheap |
| **Symptom tracking** | "Keto breath," thirst, energy | ★☆☆☆☆ Subjective | Free |

> **Target range for nutritional ketosis:** 0.5–3.0 mmol/L blood BHB. Above 3.0 mmol/L is unnecessary for most purposes (unless therapeutic epilepsy).

## Key Nutritional Considerations

### Nutrients Adequately Provided
- **High-quality protein:** From meat, fish, eggs — easily meets or exceeds requirements
- **B vitamins (especially B12):** Abundant from animal foods
- **Fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K):** From animal fats, eggs, fatty fish, butter
- **Zinc and iron (heme):** Highly bioavailable from red meat
- **Choline:** From eggs (egg yolks are one of the richest sources)
- **Selenium:** From Brazil nuts, fish, meat

### Nutrients at Risk (May Need Supplementation)
- **Fiber:** Can be low if vegetables are under-consumed — target 15–25g/day from low-carb sources
- **Magnesium:** Often suboptimal; supplement with magnesium glycinate or citrate
- **Potassium:** Needs conscious effort from avocados, greens, fish
- **Vitamin C:** Intake drops without fruit; include bell peppers, broccoli, leafy greens
- **Folate:** Reduced without grains/legumes; include leafy greens
- **Calcium:** Adequate if cheese/yogurt included; monitor if dairy-free
- **Sodium/Potassium/Magnesium:** Need conscious increase during adaptation

> **Recommended supplements:** Magnesium glycinate (200–400mg), electrolytes (sodium, potassium), omega-3 EPA/DHA (if fatty fish intake is low), and a quality multivitamin as insurance.
