# Whole30 — Food Framework

> Practical guide to what to eat, avoid, and limit during the Whole30 program. Note: Whole30 has two phases — **Elimination (Days 1-30)** and **Reintroduction (Days 31-45)**. This framework describes the elimination phase. The reintroduction protocol is detailed separately at the end.

---

## Phase 1: Elimination (Days 1-30) — Macronutrient Targets

Whole30 does not prescribe specific macronutrient ratios. Instead, it encourages eating whole foods to satiety. However, the food composition naturally produces:

| Macronutrient | Typical Range (% of total calories) | Notes |
|---------------|:----------------------------:|-------|
| **Carbohydrates** | 20–35% | From vegetables, fruits, potatoes; no grains or legumes |
| **Protein** | 25–35% | From meat, fish, eggs at every meal |
| **Fat** | 35–45% | From olive oil, avocado, nuts, coconut oil, ghee, animal fats |
| **Fiber** | 25–40 g/day | From vegetables, fruits, nuts (no grain/legume fiber) |
| **Sodium** | Variable | No restriction specified, but no processed foods means naturally lower |

> Whole30 explicitly discourages counting calories, tracking macros, or weighing food. The philosophy is to eat whole foods to satiety and focus on food quality and how you feel, not on numbers.

## Foods to Eat Daily (The Whole30 Plate)

### The Meal Template

Whole30 provides a **meal template** (not rigid portions) for building compliant meals:

| Component | Amount per Meal (guideline) |
|-----------|:--------------------------:|
| **Protein** | 1–2 palm-sized portions (thickness and length of your palm) |
| **Vegetables** | 2–3 cups (fill the rest of your plate) |
| **Fat** | 1–2 thumb-sized portions (or a small handful of nuts/seeds, or ½ avocado) |
| **Optional: Fruit** | 1–2 fist-sized portions (moderate — don't let fruit push out vegetables) |
| **Optional: Starchy vegetable** | Especially for active individuals or those feeling low-energy |

---

### Protein Sources (Every Meal)

| Food | Examples | Serving Size (per meal) |
|------|----------|:----------------------:|
| Red meat | Beef, bison, lamb, venison, pork (unprocessed, no additives) | 1–2 palms (120–200g) |
| Poultry | Chicken, turkey, duck (all cuts) | 1–2 palms |
| Fish (fatty) | Salmon, mackerel, sardines, herring, trout | 1–2 palms |
| Fish (lean) | Cod, halibut, snapper, sea bass, tilapia | 1–2 palms |
| Seafood | Shrimp, scallops, crab, mussels, clams, oysters | 1–2 palms |
| Eggs | Chicken, duck, quail | 2–4 whole eggs |
| Deli meat (compliant) | Only if ingredients are 100% compliant — no sugar, no MSG, no carrageenan, no non-compliant preservatives | Check labels carefully |

> **Critical:** All processed meats (bacon, sausage, deli meats, jerky) must have **no added sugar** and **no non-compliant additives**. Most commercial bacon and sausage contain sugar — you must find specifically "Whole30 compliant" or "no sugar added" versions.

### Vegetables (Fill Your Plate — Unlimited)

| Food | Examples | Serving Size |
|------|----------|:------------:|
| Leafy greens | Spinach, kale, arugula, chard, romaine, collards | 2–3 cups raw / 1 cup cooked |
| Cruciferous | Broccoli, cauliflower, Brussels sprouts, cabbage | 1 cup cooked |
| Alliums | Onions, garlic, leeks, shallots, scallions | Liberal |
| Peppers | Bell peppers (all colors), chilies, jalapeños | 1 medium |
| Nightshades | Tomatoes, eggplant, tomatillos | 1 cup |
| Root vegetables | Carrots, beets, turnips, parsnips | 1 cup |
| Potatoes | **White potatoes ARE allowed on Whole30** (unlike strict Paleo) | 1 medium |
| Sweet potatoes | All varieties | 1 medium |
| Squash | Zucchini, butternut, acorn, spaghetti squash | 1 cup |
| Cucumbers | All varieties | 1 cup |
| Mushrooms | All varieties | 1 cup |
| Asparagus, green beans | Asparagus, green beans, snap peas (green beans are technically legumes but Whole30 ALLOWS them) | 1 cup |

> **Note on potatoes:** Whole30 (as of the 2017 rule update) **allows** white potatoes — this is a change from the original program and differentiates Whole30 from strict Paleo. This was changed because potatoes are a whole, unprocessed food.

> **Note on green beans, snow peas, snap peas:** These are technically legumes but are **allowed** on Whole30 because they are more pod than bean and are well-tolerated by most people.

### Fruits (Moderate — 1-2 Servings/Meal)

| Food | Serving Size |
|------|:------------:|
| Berries (all types) | 1 cup |
| Citrus (oranges, grapefruit) | 1 medium |
| Apples, pears | 1 medium |
| Bananas | 1 medium |
| Stone fruit (peaches, plums, cherries) | 1 medium / 1 cup |
| Tropical (mango, pineapple, papaya) | ½ cup |
| Grapes, melon | 1 cup |

> **Note:** Whole, fresh fruit is allowed. Fruit **juice** is only allowed as an ingredient in a recipe (e.g., lemon juice in a dressing), not as a beverage. Dried fruit is allowed in moderation but **not** as a sweetener substitute (no date-sweetened "treats").

### Healthy Fats (Every Meal)

| Food | Serving Size |
|------|:------------:|
| Extra virgin olive oil | 1–2 tbsp |
| Avocado oil | 1–2 tbsp |
| Coconut oil, coconut milk (full-fat) | 1–2 tbsp / ¼ cup |
| Ghee (clarified butter) — the ONLY dairy allowed | 1–2 tbsp |
| Tallow, lard, duck fat (unprocessed) | 1–2 tbsp |
| Avocado (whole) | ½ avocado |
| Olives | Small handful |
| Nuts (all except peanuts) | Small handful (~30g) |
| Nut butters (almond, cashew, etc. — NO added sugar or oil) | 1–2 tbsp |
| Seeds (pumpkin, sunflower, flax, chia, hemp, sesame) | 1–2 tbsp |
| Coconut (shredded, flakes, unsweetened) | Small handful |

### Approved Pantry Items (Read ALL Labels)

| Item | Allowed? | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| **Vinegar** | Most types | Apple cider, white, red wine, balsamic — no added sugar; rice vinegar allowed |
| **Coconut aminos** | ✅ | Soy sauce substitute |
| **Mustard** | ✅ (if compliant) | No added sugar, no white wine (some versions exclude) |
| **Hot sauce** | ✅ (if compliant) | No sugar, no MSG — Cholula, Tessamae's, etc. |
| **Salt** | ✅ | Any salt (iodized, sea salt, kosher, Himalayan) |
| **Herbs and spices** | ✅ | All single-ingredient dried/fresh herbs and spices |
| **Spice blends** | ⚠️ Check labels | Many contain sugar, anti-caking agents, or sulfites |
| **Coffee** | ✅ | Black or with compliant additions (coconut milk, ghee) |
| **Tea** | ✅ | No added sugar or non-compliant ingredients |
| **Vanilla extract** | ❌ | Contains alcohol (use alcohol-free vanilla or vanilla bean) |
| **Extracts** | ⚠️ Check labels | Many contain alcohol or sugar |
| **Baking soda, cream of tartar** | ✅ | For compliant cooking |

## Foods to Strictly Avoid (The "No" List — Zero Tolerance)

| Category | What's Out | Detail |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| **Added sugar (ALL forms)** | Cane sugar, brown sugar, powdered sugar, honey, maple syrup, agave, coconut sugar, date sugar, stevia, monk fruit, Splenda, Equal, xylitol, erythritol, all artificial sweeteners | Read every label — sugar is in bacon, broth, sauces, spice blends |
| **Alcohol (ALL forms)** | Wine, beer, spirits, liqueurs, cooking wine, alcohol-based extracts (vanilla extract) | Zero tolerance — no exceptions, even for cooking |
| **Grains (ALL)** | Wheat, rice, oats, corn, barley, rye, quinoa, buckwheat, amaranth, millet, sorghum, teff, sprouted grains, all flours (wheat, rice, almond, coconut, etc.) | Including all products made from grains |
| **Legumes (ALL)** | Beans (all types), lentils, chickpeas, split peas, peanuts, peanut butter, soy (tofu, tempeh, edamame, miso, soy sauce), peas (EXCEPT green beans, snow peas, snap peas which are allowed) | Including sprouted legumes |
| **Dairy (ALL)** | Cow, goat, and sheep milk, cheese, yogurt, butter, cream, sour cream, whey, casein, lactose, kefir, ice cream | Ghee (clarified butter with milk solids removed) is the ONLY exception |
| **Carrageenan** | This specific additive (found in non-dairy milks, deli meats, toothpaste) | Must check all labels |
| **MSG** | Monosodium glutamate and related forms (yeast extract, hydrolyzed protein, textured protein, autolyzed yeast) | Hidden in many processed foods, broths, and seasonings |
| **Sulfites** | Added sulfites (naturally occurring in wine is irrelevant — all alcohol is excluded anyway) | Found in dried fruit (if sulfited), some processed foods, vinegar (some) |
| **Baked goods & treats** | Even with compliant ingredients — no pancakes, waffles, muffins, cookies, brownies, bread, "nice cream," energy balls, protein bars | The "no recreating" rule |
| **Junk food** | Chips, candy, pretzels, crackers, commercial snacks — even if "paleo" or "gluten-free" | No processed snack foods |

### The "No Recreating" Rule (Detailed)

You may NOT make "compliant versions" of off-plan foods, including:

| Don't Do This | Why |
|---------------|-----|
| Cauliflower "rice" | Recreating rice form |
| Zucchini "noodles" / spiralized vegetables as pasta | Recreating pasta form |
| Banana "ice cream" (blended frozen banana) | Recreating dessert |
| Coconut-flour "pancakes" or "pizza crust" | Recreating baked goods |
| Date-sweetened "energy balls" or "truffles" | Recreating candy |
| "Paleo" cookies or muffins | Recreating treats |
| Cashew "cheese" sauce | Recreating dairy product |

> **Exception:** You CAN use ingredients that are technically compliant (like riced cauliflower in a stir-fry, or blended cashews in a soup for creaminess) — the rule is about your **intent**. If the purpose is to create a "pretend" version of an off-plan food, it's out. If it's just a natural way to prepare a compliant ingredient, it's fine.

## Sample Daily Meal Plan (Elimination Phase)

### Breakfast
- 3 scrambled eggs cooked in ghee with spinach, mushrooms, and diced tomatoes
- ½ avocado
- Handful of berries
- Black coffee or herbal tea
- *Or:* Sweet potato hash with ground beef, onions, bell peppers, topped with fried eggs

### Lunch
- Large mixed greens salad with grilled chicken or salmon
- Cherry tomatoes, cucumber, red onion, olives, sliced almonds
- Dressing: olive oil, compliant mustard, lemon juice, herbs
- *Or:* Tuna salad (mixed with avocado instead of mayo) over mixed greens
- Side of roasted sweet potato

### Snack (optional — Whole30 discourages snacking but allows if genuinely hungry)
- Handful of almonds and an apple
- *Or:* Carrots and celery with guacamole or compliant ranch
- *Or:* Hard-boiled egg
- *Or:* Olives and a piece of fruit

### Dinner
- Pan-seared steak or grilled salmon
- Roasted Brussels sprouts or broccoli with olive oil
- Baked sweet potato with ghee
- Side salad with olive oil and vinegar
- *Or:* Slow-cooked pork shoulder with roasted vegetables and plantains

### Dessert
- **There is no dessert on Whole30.**
- If you need something after dinner: a piece of fresh fruit, or a cup of herbal tea
- Whole30 explicitly discourages using fruit as a dessert substitute if it's feeding a sugar craving

## Weekly Meal Structure (Sample)

| Day | Breakfast | Lunch | Dinner |
|-----|-----------|-------|--------|
| Monday | Eggs with avocado and berries | Chicken salad with olive oil dressing | Grilled salmon with roasted broccoli and sweet potato |
| Tuesday | Sweet potato hash with eggs and sausage (compliant) | Leftover salmon with salad | Beef stir-fry (coconut aminos) with peppers and zucchini |
| Wednesday | Frittata with mushrooms and spinach | Leftover beef stir-fry | Roast chicken with Brussels sprouts and potatoes |
| Thursday | Scrambled eggs with smoked salmon and avocado | Chicken salad (leftover) with compliant mayo | Pork tenderloin with mashed sweet potato and asparagus |
| Friday | Fried eggs over sautéed greens and tomato | Tuna salad over greens with olives | Grilled shrimp with zucchini noodles (in a sauce, not "pasta") and tomato |
| Saturday | Omelet with compliant ham and vegetables | Leftover shrimp and vegetables | Slow-cooked beef stew with carrots, celery, and potatoes |
| Sunday | Baked eggs in tomato sauce (shakshuka, compliant) | Leftover beef stew | Roasted pork with butternut squash and green beans |

> **Key strategies for success:**
> - **Meal prep is essential** — cook large batches on weekends
> - **Always have compliant emergency food** (nuts, hard-boiled eggs, compliant jerky)
> - **Read EVERY label** — sugar and non-compliant additives are hidden in surprising places
> - **Plan for social situations** — bring your own food, eat before events, or suggest compliant restaurants

## Phase 2: The Reintroduction Protocol (Days 31-45)

After 30 days of strict elimination, systematically reintroduce food groups. **This is not optional** — the reintroduction is what makes Whole30 a diagnostic tool rather than just a restrictive diet.

### Reintroduction Schedule

| Day | Reintroduce | What to Eat | Observation Period |
|-----|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Day 31** | **Legumes** | Have some peanut butter with breakfast, soy sauce with lunch, kidney beans with dinner | Observe for 2 days |
| **Day 32** | *(strict Whole30)* | Return to 100% compliant eating | Monitor for delayed reactions |
| **Day 33** | *(strict Whole30)* | Continue compliant eating | Last day of legume observation |
| **Day 34** | **Non-gluten grains** | Have corn tortilla with breakfast, rice with lunch, oatmeal with dinner | Observe for 2 days |
| **Day 35** | *(strict Whole30)* | Return to 100% compliant eating | Monitor |
| **Day 36** | *(strict Whole30)* | Continue compliant eating | Last day of grain observation |
| **Day 37** | **Dairy** | Have yogurt with breakfast, cheese with lunch, ice cream with dinner | Observe for 2 days |
| **Day 38** | *(strict Whole30)* | Return to 100% compliant eating | Monitor |
| **Day 39** | *(strict Whole30)* | Continue compliant eating | Last day of dairy observation |
| **Day 40** | **Gluten grains** | Have whole wheat bread with breakfast, beer with lunch, pasta with dinner | Observe for 2 days |
| **Day 41** | *(strict Whole30)* | Return to 100% compliant eating | Monitor |
| **Day 42** | *(strict Whole30)* | Continue compliant eating | Last day of gluten observation |

> **Optional additional reintro days:** Sugar/alcohol can be reintroduced separately, or you may choose to evaluate them alongside other groups (e.g., having wine with your dairy reintro day).

### What to Observe During Reintroduction

For each food group, monitor and document:

| Symptom Category | Examples |
|-----------------|----------|
| **Digestive** | Bloating, gas, cramping, diarrhea, constipation, acid reflux |
| **Energy** | Fatigue, energy crash, lethargy, hyperactivity |
| **Mood** | Irritability, anxiety, brain fog, mood swings |
| **Skin** | Acne, rash, redness, itchiness, eczema flare |
| **Sleep** | Difficulty falling asleep, waking, poor quality |
| **Physical** | Joint pain, headache, sinus congestion, inflammation |
| **Cravings** | Increased cravings, especially for sugar |

### Interpreting Results

| Reaction | Interpretation | Recommendation |
|----------|---------------|----------------|
| **No reaction** | You tolerate this food well | Can include in your regular diet |
| **Mild reaction** | Mild sensitivity | Eat occasionally, monitor |
| **Strong reaction** | Significant sensitivity | Avoid or minimize long-term |
| **Immediate reaction** | Possible allergy | Consult a physician |

## Key Nutritional Considerations

### Nutrients Adequately Provided
- **Protein:** High intake from meat, fish, eggs
- **Healthy fats:** From olive oil, avocado, nuts, coconut, ghee
- **Fiber:** From vegetables, fruits, nuts, potatoes
- **Micronutrients:** Generally high from diverse whole foods — vitamin B12, iron, zinc, vitamin C, vitamin K, potassium

### Nutrients at Risk of Deficiency (During 30 Days)
| Nutrient | Risk Level | Reason | Solution |
|----------|:---------:|--------|----------|
| **Calcium** | **High** | No dairy for 30 days | Sardines with bones, broccoli, kale, almonds, fortified coconut/almond milk |
| **Vitamin D** | Moderate | If limited fatty fish or sun | Salmon, egg yolks, sun exposure |
| **Iodine** | Low-Moderate | Limited seafood/dairy | Include fish, shellfish, seaweed |
| **Carbohydrate** (athletes) | Moderate | Low-carb from food restrictions | Include potatoes, sweet potatoes, fruit |
| **Convenience** | High | No processed/quick foods | Meal prep is essential; this is a time-cost consideration |

> **Note:** Since Whole30 is only 30 days, nutritional deficiencies are unlikely to develop in otherwise healthy adults. The concern is more about quality of life (social difficulty, cooking burden) than clinical deficiency.
