# Plant-Based / Vegan / Vegetarian Diet — Food Framework

> Practical guide to what to eat, avoid, and limit on plant-based, vegan, and vegetarian diets, including macronutrient targets, portion guidelines, and sample meal structures for each sub-type.

---

## Macronutrient Targets

| Macronutrient | Vegan / WFPB Target | Lacto-Ovo Vegetarian Target | Notes |
|---------------|:-------------------:|:--------------------------:|-------|
| **Carbohydrates** | 50–65% | 45–60% | Primarily from whole grains, legumes, vegetables, fruits |
| **Protein** | 12–18% | 13–18% | From legumes, soy foods, nuts, seeds, whole grains (+ eggs/dairy for lacto-ovo) |
| **Fat** | 15–30% | 25–35% | From nuts, seeds, avocado, olive oil; WFPB purists target lower end |
| **Saturated fat** | <7% | <8% | Naturally very low in vegan diets |
| **Fiber** | 35–55 g/day | 30–45 g/day | Among the highest of any dietary pattern |
| **Sodium** | <2,300 mg/day | <2,300 mg/day | Higher risk of excessive sodium from processed plant-based foods |

> **Note on macronutrient variability:** There is no single "correct" macro ratio for plant-based diets. WFPB purists (e.g., Ornish, Esselstyn, McDougall) target fat at <10–15% of calories, while moderate plant-based eaters may consume 25–35% fat from nuts, seeds, avocado, and oils. The common thread is **whole-food, minimally processed plant foods** as the calorie base.

> **Important:** Vegan diets are naturally low in certain nutrients. See the **supplementation table** in [04_Health_Outcomes.md](./04_Health_Outcomes.md) for mandatory and recommended supplements.

## Foods to Eat Daily (The Base)

### Vegetables (5+ servings/day)
Eat a wide variety, focusing on color diversity and volume:

| Food | Examples | Serving Size |
|------|----------|:------------:|
| Leafy greens | Spinach, kale, collards, Swiss chard, arugula, romaine, bok choy | 1 cup raw / ½ cup cooked |
| Cruciferous | Broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage, Brussels sprouts, radishes | ½ cup cooked |
| Alliums | Onions, garlic, leeks, shallots, scallions | ½ onion / 1-2 cloves garlic |
| Nightshades | Tomatoes, bell peppers, eggplant | 1 medium / ½ cup |
| Root vegetables | Carrots, beets, parsnips, turnips, radishes | ½ cup cooked |
| Starchy vegetables | Potatoes, sweet potatoes, butternut squash, pumpkin | ½ cup cooked |
| Cucurbits | Zucchini, cucumber, summer squash | ½ cup |
| Mushrooms | Button, cremini, shiitake, portobello, oyster | ½ cup cooked |

### Fruits (3–5 servings/day)
| Food | Examples | Serving Size |
|------|----------|:------------:|
| Berries | Strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, blackberries, cranberries | 1 cup |
| Citrus | Oranges, grapefruit, lemons, limes, tangerines | 1 medium |
| Tropical | Banana, mango, pineapple, papaya, kiwi | 1 medium / ½ cup |
| Stone fruit | Peaches, plums, apricots, cherries, nectarines | 1 medium |
| Pome fruit | Apples, pears | 1 medium |
| Grapes, melons | All varieties | 1 cup |
| Dried fruit | Raisins, dates, figs, prunes (moderate — calorie dense) | ¼ cup |

### Legumes & Beans (2–3 servings/day — the protein powerhouse)
| Food | Serving Size | Protein |
|------|:------------:|:-------:|
| Lentils (red, green, brown, black, French) | ½ cup cooked | ~9g |
| Chickpeas (garbanzo beans) | ½ cup cooked | ~7g |
| Black beans, kidney beans, pinto beans | ½ cup cooked | ~7-8g |
| Navy beans, cannellini, great northern | ½ cup cooked | ~8g |
| Edamame (young soybeans) | ½ cup | ~9g |
| Split peas | ½ cup cooked | ~8g |
| Hummus | ¼ cup | ~5g |
| Tofu (firm/extra firm) | ½ cup (100g) | ~10-15g |
| Tempeh | ½ cup (100g) | ~15-20g |

> **Legumes are the cornerstone of plant-based protein.** Aim for at least 3 cups of legumes per week (or ~½ cup daily). Combining legumes with whole grains (e.g., rice and beans, hummus and pita) provides a complete amino acid profile, though precise combining at every meal is not necessary if total protein intake is adequate.

### Whole Grains (3–6 servings/day)
| Food | Serving Size |
|------|:------------:|
| Brown rice, wild rice | ½ cup cooked |
| Quinoa | ½ cup cooked |
| Oats (rolled or steel-cut) | ½ cup cooked |
| Barley, farro, wheat berries | ½ cup cooked |
| Buckwheat, millet, amaranth | ½ cup cooked |
| Whole grain bread (100% whole wheat, sprouted) | 1 slice |
| Whole grain pasta | ½–1 cup cooked |
| Corn (on cob or kernels) | ½ cup |
| Popcorn (air-popped) | 3 cups |

### Soy Foods (1–3 servings/day — safe and recommended)
| Food | Serving Size | Notes |
|------|:------------:|-------|
| Tofu (silken, firm, extra firm) | ½ cup | Versatile protein source; calcium-set tofu is high in calcium |
| Tempeh | ½ cup | Fermented; high protein; probiotics |
| Edamame | ½ cup | Whole soybean; high protein and fiber |
| Soy milk (unsweetened, fortified) | 1 cup | Best nutritional profile of plant milks (protein, calcium, B12 if fortified) |
| Natto, miso | 1–2 tbsp | Fermented; high vitamin K2 |

> **Soy safety note:** Current scientific consensus (American Cancer Society, AICR, AND) confirms that **1–3 servings/day of whole or minimally processed soy foods** (tofu, tempeh, edamame, soy milk) is safe and likely beneficial. Avoid isolated soy protein in large amounts from ultra-processed sources. Soy does NOT feminize men or increase breast cancer risk at normal intakes.

### Nuts & Seeds (1–2 handfuls/day, ~30–60g)
| Food | Serving Size | Key Nutrients |
|------|:------------:|---------------|
| Walnuts | 30g (14 halves) | Omega-3 (ALA) |
| Flaxseeds (ground) | 1–2 tbsp | Omega-3 (ALA), lignans |
| Chia seeds | 1–2 tbsp | Omega-3 (ALA), fiber, calcium |
| Hemp seeds | 2–3 tbsp | Complete protein, omega-3 and omega-6 |
| Almonds | 30g (~23) | Calcium, vitamin E, protein |
| Brazil nuts | 1–2 nuts | Selenium (do not exceed — selenium toxicity risk) |
| Pumpkin seeds | 30g | Zinc, magnesium, iron |
| Sunflower seeds | 30g | Vitamin E, selenium |
| Cashews, pistachios, pecans, macadamia | 30g | Healthy fats |
| Tahini (sesame paste) | 1–2 tbsp | Calcium |
| Peanut butter (natural) | 2 tbsp | Protein (technically a legume) |

> **Omega-3 note:** Plant-based diets provide alpha-linolenic acid (ALA) from flaxseeds, chia, hemp, and walnuts. ALA must be converted to EPA and DHA (the active omega-3 forms), but conversion is inefficient (~5–10% for EPA, <1% for DHA). Vegans should consider an **algae-derived DHA/EPA supplement** (200–300 mg/day), especially during pregnancy/lactation.

### Healthy Fats (daily, moderate)
| Food | Serving Size |
|------|:------------:|
| Avocado | ¼–½ avocado |
| Extra virgin olive oil | 1–2 tbsp (WFPB purists may exclude) |
| Nut butters (almond, cashew, peanut — natural) | 1–2 tbsp |
| Coconut (fresh or unsweetened) | Moderate — high saturated fat |
| Dark chocolate (70%+) | 1–2 squares (also a polyphenol source) |

### Flavorings (liberal use)
- **Herbs:** basil, oregano, thyme, rosemary, sage, parsley, cilantro, mint, dill
- **Spices:** turmeric, cumin, coriander, paprika, cinnamon, ginger, cardamom, black pepper
- **Umami:** nutritional yeast (also a B12 source if fortified), miso, tamari/soy sauce, liquid aminos, mushrooms, tomato paste
- **Acids:** lemon juice, lime juice, apple cider vinegar, balsamic vinegar, rice vinegar

## Foods to Limit / Minimize

| Food | Reason |
|------|--------|
| **Red meat** (beef, pork, lamb) | Excluded in all sub-types (except flexible plant-based, minimal) |
| **Processed meats** (sausages, bacon, deli meats, hot dogs) | AVOID — WHO Group 1 carcinogen |
| **Poultry** | Excluded in vegan; optional/minimal in flexible plant-based |
| **Fish/seafood** | Excluded in vegan and vegetarian; optional in flexible plant-based |
| **Ultra-processed vegan foods** | Many "vegan" junk foods are highly processed, high in sodium, refined oils, and additives |
| **Refined grains** (white bread, white rice, regular pasta) | Replace with whole grain versions |
| **Refined sugar & sweets** | Minimize — treats only |
| **Sugary drinks** (soda, sweetened plant milks, juice with added sugar) | AVOID |
| **Fried foods** (fries, fried snacks) | Minimize even if vegan |
| **Industrial seed oils** (in large amounts) | WFPB purists minimize all extracted oils |
| **High-sodium sauces** | Some soy sauce, vegan bouillon can be very high in sodium |

## Daily Eating Pattern — Sample Day (Vegan)

### Breakfast
- Oatmeal made with soy milk, topped with mixed berries, ground flaxseed, walnuts, and a drizzle of maple syrup
- *Or:* Tofu scramble with spinach, mushrooms, bell peppers, turmeric, and nutritional yeast, served with whole grain toast
- Coffee or green tea

### Lunch
- Buddha bowl: quinoa, roasted chickpeas, sweet potato, kale, avocado, red cabbage, tahini-lemon dressing
- *Or:* Large lentil and vegetable soup with whole grain bread and a side salad
- *Or:* Chickpea salad sandwich (mashed chickpeas, vegan mayo or avocado, celery, red onion, lemon) on whole grain bread

### Snack (optional)
- Apple with almond butter
- *Or:* Handful of mixed nuts and dried fruit
- *Or:* Edamame with sea salt
- *Or:* Carrot and cucumber sticks with hummus

### Dinner
- Tempeh stir-fry with broccoli, bell peppers, snap peas, mushrooms, and brown rice, seasoned with ginger, garlic, tamari, and sesame seeds
- *Or:* Black bean and sweet potato enchiladas with avocado and salsa
- *Or:* Lentil shepherd's pie with mashed potato topping and side of green beans

### Dessert (optional)
- Fresh fruit
- *Or:* Dark chocolate (70%+) with a handful of almonds
- *Or:* Chia pudding made with plant milk and topped with berries

## Daily Eating Pattern — Sample Day (Lacto-Ovo Vegetarian)

### Breakfast
- Greek yogurt parfait with oats, walnuts, honey, and blueberries
- *Or:* Vegetable omelet with feta, spinach, and tomato, with whole grain toast
- Coffee or tea

### Lunch
- Caprese panini (mozzarella, tomato, basil) on whole grain bread with side salad
- *Or:* Lentil soup with a slice of cheese toast and mixed green salad
- *Or:* Egg salad sandwich with lettuce and tomato

### Snack (optional)
- String cheese and an apple
- *Or:* Handful of almonds
- *Or:* Cottage cheese with peach slices

### Dinner
- Eggplant parmesan with whole wheat pasta and side salad
- *Or:* Chickpea curry with yogurt raita and brown rice
- *Or:* Veggie burger (black bean or lentil-based) with sweet potato fries

## Weekly Meal Structure (Vegan)

| Day | Breakfast | Lunch | Dinner |
|-----|-----------|-------|--------|
| Monday | Oatmeal with berries, flax, walnuts | Lentil soup with whole grain bread | Tofu stir-fry with brown rice and vegetables |
| Tuesday | Tofu scramble with spinach and nutritional yeast | Chickpea salad sandwich with side salad | Black bean tacos with avocado, salsa, cilantro |
| Wednesday | Smoothie bowl with banana, berries, granola, chia | Quinoa Buddha bowl with roasted vegetables and tahini | Lentil dal with basmati rice and saag |
| Thursday | Oatmeal with banana, peanut butter | Edamame and vegetable sushi rolls | Tempeh and sweet potato curry with coconut milk |
| Friday | Whole grain toast with avocado and tomato | Minestrone soup with whole grain crackers | Vegan shepherd's pie (lentils, mashed potato) |
| Saturday | Pancakes (flax egg) with maple syrup and berries | Falafel wrap with hummus, tabbouleh, and salad | Pasta primavera with marinara and nutritional yeast |
| Sunday | Chia pudding with mango and coconut | Buddha bowl with tempeh, kale, quinoa, tahini | Stuffed bell peppers with rice, beans, corn, spices |

> **Note on meal planning:** The key to successful plant-based eating is **batch cooking** of staples — cook large batches of grains (rice, quinoa), legumes (lentils, chickpeas), and roasted vegetables on the weekend for quick assembly of meals during the week.

## The Plant-Based Plate Method

A simple visual guide used by plant-based dietitians:

| Plate Section | Proportion | Foods |
|---------------|:----------:|-------|
| **Vegetables and fruits** | ½ plate | Leafy greens, cruciferous, colorful vegetables, berries, citrus |
| **Whole grains and starchy vegetables** | ¼ plate | Brown rice, quinoa, sweet potato, oats, whole wheat pasta |
| **Legumes and plant proteins** | ¼ plate | Beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh, edamame |
| **Bonus: healthy fats** | Side/addition | Nuts, seeds, avocado, olive oil (moderate) |

> This plate method naturally produces a diet of ~55–65% carbohydrate, ~12–18% protein, ~20–30% fat — consistent with well-designed WFPB macro targets.

## Key Nutritional Considerations

### Nutrients Adequately Provided (Plant-based diets excel)
- **Fiber:** 35–55g/day (among the highest of any diet pattern) — far exceeding the 25–30g recommendation
- **Antioxidants and polyphenols:** Extremely high from diverse plant foods
- **Potassium:** High from fruits, vegetables, legumes — supports blood pressure
- **Magnesium:** From whole grains, nuts, seeds, leafy greens
- **Folate:** High from legumes, leafy greens
- **Vitamin C:** High from fruits and vegetables — also enhances iron absorption
- **Vitamin K (K1):** High from leafy greens
- **Beta-carotene (provitamin A):** High from orange/yellow vegetables

### Nutrients Requiring Attention (see 04_Health_Outcomes.md for details)

| Nutrient | Risk Level | Solution |
|----------|:---------:|----------|
| **Vitamin B12** | 🔴 HIGH (vegans) | **Mandatory supplement** (250–1000 mcg/day or 2500 mcg/week); fortified foods (nutritional yeast, plant milks) |
| **Vitamin D** | 🟡 MODERATE | Fortified foods, supplements (1000–2000 IU/day), sensible sun exposure |
| **Omega-3 (DHA/EPA)** | 🟡 MODERATE | Algae-based DHA/EPA supplement (200–300 mg/day); maximize ALA (flax, chia, walnuts) |
| **Iron** | 🟡 MODERATE | Pair plant iron (legumes, leafy greens, fortified cereals) with vitamin C; cook in cast iron |
| **Zinc** | 🟡 MODERATE | Pumpkin seeds, chickpeas, lentils, cashews, hemp seeds; soak/sprout to improve bioavailability |
| **Calcium** | 🟡 MODERATE | Calcium-set tofu, fortified plant milks, leafy greens (collards, kale), tahini, fortified orange juice |
| **Iodine** | 🟡 MODERATE | Iodized salt (moderate), seaweed (moderate — avoid excess), supplement if needed |
| **Selenium** | 🟢 LOW | 1–2 Brazil nuts/day covers requirements |
| **Protein** | 🟢 LOW (if planned) | Combine diverse protein sources; aim for ~0.8–1.0g/kg body weight |

## Plant Milk Comparison

| Milk (1 cup) | Protein | Calcium | B12 | Calories | Notes |
|-------------|:-------:|:-------:|:---:|:--------:|-------|
| **Soy milk (fortified)** | 7–8g | 300mg* | 1.2mcg* | 80–100 | Best nutritional match to cow's milk |
| **Pea milk (Ripple)** | 8g | 350mg* | — | 70–100 | High protein; good for allergy avoidance |
| **Oat milk** | 3–4g | 350mg* | — | 120–150 | Low protein; creamy; environmental concerns |
| **Almond milk** | 1g | 450mg* | — | 30–40 | Very low protein; low calorie |
| **Coconut milk** | 0–1g | 450mg* | — | 40–80 | High saturated fat; low protein |
| **Rice milk** | 0.7g | 300mg* | — | 110–130 | High glycemic index; low protein |
| **Cow's milk (ref)** | 8g | 300mg | 1.1mcg | 100–150 | (Not vegan; shown for comparison) |

*Fortified — check labels, as fortification varies by brand and country.

> **Recommendation:** **Fortified soy milk** or **pea milk** are the best plant milk choices for nutritional adequacy, as they provide comparable protein to cow's milk. Always choose **unsweetened** varieties.
