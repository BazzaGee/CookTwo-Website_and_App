# Pritikin Diet — Food Framework

> Practical guide to what to eat, avoid, and limit on the Pritikin Diet, including macronutrient targets, portion guidelines, and sample meal structures.

---

## Macronutrient Targets

| Macronutrient | Target (% of total calories) | Notes |
|---------------|:----------------------------:|-------|
| **Carbohydrates** | 70–80% | Exclusively from complex, unrefined sources — whole grains, vegetables, fruits, legumes |
| **Protein** | 10–15% | Primarily plant-based; limited lean animal protein |
| **Fat** | 5–10% | Extremely low — no added fats whatsoever |
| **Saturated fat** | <3% | Near-zero |
| **Cholesterol** | <100 mg/day | Essentially zero — no eggs, dairy, or fatty meat |
| **Fiber** | 35–45 g/day | Very high — from unlimited plant foods |
| **Sodium** | <1,500 mg/day | Minimize added salt; rely on herbs/spices |

> The Pritikin macro profile is one of the most extreme of any mainstream diet — lower in fat than even the Ornish Program (which targets <10%) in practice, because the Pritikin guidelines eliminate all added fats while Ornish permits small amounts.

## Foods to Eat Daily (The Base — Unlimited)

### Vegetables (7+ servings/day — eat freely)

The Pritikin approach encourages eating vegetables in abundance. There is no portion limit.

| Food | Examples | Serving Size |
|------|----------|:------------:|
| Leafy greens | Spinach, kale, collards, Swiss chard, romaine, arugula | 1 cup raw / ½ cup cooked |
| Cruciferous | Broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage, Brussels sprouts, bok choy | ½ cup cooked |
| Alliums | Onions, garlic, leeks, shallots | ½ cup cooked |
| Peppers | Bell peppers (all colors), chili peppers | 1 medium |
| Root vegetables | Carrots, beets, turnips, parsnips, radishes | ½ cup cooked |
| Squash | Summer squash, zucchini, winter squash (butternut, acorn) | ½ cup cooked |
| Tomatoes | Fresh, canned (no added salt/sugar), puree, paste | 1 medium / ½ cup |
| Nightshades | Eggplant, tomatillos | ½ cup cooked |
| Other | Asparagus, green beans, celery, cucumber, mushrooms, artichokes, fennel | ½ cup cooked |

### Fruits (4+ servings/day)

| Food | Examples | Serving Size |
|------|----------|:------------:|
| Berries | Strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, blackberries | 1 cup |
| Citrus | Oranges, grapefruit, lemons, limes, mandarins | 1 medium |
| Stone fruit | Peaches, plums, nectarines, apricots, cherries | 1 medium |
| Tropical | Bananas, pineapple, mango, papaya, kiwi | 1 medium |
| Melons | Watermelon, cantaloupe, honeydew | 1 cup |
| Apples, pears | Fresh, with skin | 1 medium |
| Grapes | Fresh | 1 cup |
| Dried fruit | Unsweetened only (no added sugar or oil) | ¼ cup |

### Whole Grains (5+ servings/day)

| Food | Examples | Serving Size |
|------|----------|:------------:|
| Brown rice | All types — long grain, short grain, basmati | ½–1 cup cooked |
| Oats | Rolled, steel-cut (not instant with added sugar) | ½ cup cooked |
| Barley | Hulled or pearled | ½ cup cooked |
| Quinoa | All colors | ½ cup cooked |
| Bulgur | | ½ cup cooked |
| Millet | | ½ cup cooked |
| Whole wheat | Whole wheat bread (no added fat), whole wheat pasta | 1 slice / ½ cup cooked |
| Corn | Corn on the cob, cornmeal/polenta, popcorn (air-popped, no oil/butter) | 1 ear / ½ cup |
| Buckwheat | Groats, soba noodles (100% buckwheat) | ½ cup cooked |
| 100% whole grain cereals | No added sugar or fat (e.g., shredded wheat, plain oatmeal) | ½–1 cup |

### Legumes & Beans (2+ servings/day — daily)

| Food | Serving Size |
|------|:------------:|
| Lentils (all varieties) | ½ cup cooked |
| Chickpeas (garbanzo beans) | ½ cup cooked |
| Black beans | ½ cup cooked |
| Kidney beans | ½ cup cooked |
| Navy beans, pinto beans, cannellini | ½ cup cooked |
| Split peas | ½ cup cooked |
| Edamame (whole soybeans) | ½ cup |
| Tofu (non-fried, no added fat) | ½ cup |
| Tempeh (plain) | ½ cup |

### Starchy Vegetables (1–2 servings/day)

| Food | Serving Size |
|------|:------------:|
| Potatoes (white) | 1 medium — boiled or baked (not fried) |
| Sweet potatoes | 1 medium |
| Winter squash | ½ cup cooked |
| Corn | ½ cup / 1 ear |

### Herbs & Spices (liberal use)

- Use unlimited: basil, oregano, rosemary, thyme, sage, parsley, cilantro, mint, dill, bay leaf
- Spices: turmeric, cinnamon, cumin, coriander, paprika, black pepper, ginger, nutmeg, cardamom
- Alliums: garlic, onion, shallots, leeks — used liberally for flavor (no fat needed)
- Acid: lemon juice, lime juice, vinegar (all types) — critical for flavor without fat
- Salt substitutes: potassium chloride, herb blends, nutritional yeast

### Beverages

| Beverage | Status |
|----------|--------|
| Water | ✅ Primary beverage |
| Coffee | ✅ Black only (no cream, milk, or sugar) |
| Tea | ✅ All types (black, green, herbal) — unsweetened |
| Sparkling water | ✅ |
| 100% fruit juice | ⚠️ Limit to 4 oz/day (high natural sugar, no fiber) |
| Alcohol | ⚠️ Minimize or avoid — no more than 1 drink/week |

## Foods to Eat in Moderation (Limited Portions)

### Lean Animal Protein (max 3.5 oz/serving, up to 1 serving/day)

| Food | Serving Size | Frequency |
|------|:------------:|-----------|
| White fish (cod, flounder, sole, halibut, snapper) | 3.5 oz (100g) | Up to 4 servings/week |
| Shellfish (shrimp, scallops, crab) | 3.5 oz (100g) | Occasionally |
| Skinless chicken breast | 3.5 oz (100g) | Up to 2–3 servings/week |
| Skinless turkey breast | 3.5 oz (100g) | Up to 2–3 servings/week |
| Egg whites | 2 whites | Occasionally (whole eggs excluded) |

> **Note:** The Pritikin Program permits but does not require animal protein. The diet can be followed as fully plant-based. The allowance of small amounts of fish and poultry distinguishes Pritikin from the fully vegan Ornish and Esselstyn programs.

### Sodium / Salt

- Minimize added salt to <1,500 mg/day total sodium
- Use herbs, spices, lemon, vinegar instead
- Choose low-sodium or no-salt-added versions of canned products

## Foods to Avoid Entirely

### All Added Fats and Oils
| Food | Reason |
|------|--------|
| **All cooking oils** (olive, canola, coconut, vegetable, sesame, etc.) | Pure fat — violates <10% fat target |
| **Butter, ghee** | Saturated fat and cholesterol |
| **Margarine** | Processed fat |
| **Lard, tallow, shortening** | Saturated/trans fat |
| **Mayonnaise** | Oil-based |
| **Salad dressings** (oil-based) | Replace with fat-free dressings (vinegar, lemon, herbs) |
| **Pesto** | Oil + cheese + nuts |
| **Nut butters** (peanut butter, almond butter) | High fat |

### High-Fat Plant Foods
| Food | Reason |
|------|--------|
| **Nuts** (all types) | 50-75% fat by calories |
| **Seeds** (sunflower, pumpkin, sesame, flax) | High fat (small amounts of ground flaxseed permitted for omega-3) |
| **Avocado** | ~75% fat |
| **Coconut** (fresh, dried, milk, oil) | Very high saturated fat |
| **Olives** | High fat (despite being heart-healthy in moderate-fat diets) |
| **Soybeans / edamame** (large amounts) | Moderate fat — small portions OK |
| **Dark chocolate** | High fat |

### Fatty Meats and Full-Fat Dairy
| Food | Reason |
|------|--------|
| **Red meat** (beef, pork, lamb) | Saturated fat and cholesterol |
| **Processed meat** (sausage, bacon, deli meat, hot dogs) | Saturated fat, sodium, preservatives |
| **Dark poultry meat / skin** | High fat |
| **Whole eggs / egg yolks** | Cholesterol (212mg per yolk) |
| **Full-fat milk, cheese, cream, yogurt, ice cream** | Saturated fat and cholesterol |
| **Butter** | Saturated fat and cholesterol |

### Refined Carbohydrates
| Food | Reason |
|------|--------|
| **White flour** (white bread, regular pasta, crackers) | Stripped of fiber and nutrients |
| **White rice** | Lower fiber than brown rice |
| **Refined sugar** (table sugar, candy, pastries, desserts) | Empty calories, glycemic spike |
| **Sugary drinks** (soda, sports drinks, sweetened juice) | Empty calories |
| **Syrups** (corn syrup, maple syrup, honey, agave) | Concentrated sugar |

### Processed and Ultra-Processed Foods
| Food | Reason |
|------|--------|
| **Fast food** | Typically high in fat, sodium, and refined carbs |
| **Frozen meals** (most) | Added fat and sodium |
| **Snack foods** (chips, crackers, pretzels) | Added fat and/or refined flour |
| **Baked goods** | Fat + sugar + refined flour |

## Sample Daily Meal Plan

### Breakfast
- Large bowl of steel-cut oatmeal with cinnamon and berries
- Sliced banana and chopped apple
- Black coffee or herbal tea
- *Or:* Whole grain pancakes (no oil or butter) with fruit compote; air-popped popcorn on the side

### Lunch
- Large salad: mixed greens, tomatoes, cucumber, bell peppers, chickpeas, corn, beans
- Dressing: balsamic vinegar + lemon juice + herbs (no oil)
- ½ cup brown rice or quinoa
- *Or:* Lentil and vegetable soup with a large whole grain roll (no butter); side salad

### Snack (optional)
- Fresh fruit (apple, banana, orange)
- *Or:* Raw vegetables (carrots, celery, bell peppers) with fat-free bean dip / salsa
- *Or:* Air-popped popcorn (no butter)

### Dinner
- Steamed or baked white fish (3.5 oz) with lemon and herbs
- Large portion of steamed vegetables (broccoli, carrots, green beans)
- Baked sweet potato
- *Or (vegetarian):* Chickpea and vegetable curry (no oil — sauté with water or broth) over brown rice; side salad with vinegar dressing

### Dessert (optional)
- Fresh fruit salad
- *Or:* Baked apple with cinnamon and raisins

## Weekly Meal Structure

| Day | Breakfast | Lunch | Dinner |
|-----|-----------|-------|--------|
| Monday | Oatmeal, berries, banana | Lentil soup, whole grain roll, salad | Brown rice, black beans, steamed broccoli |
| Tuesday | Whole grain cereal, fruit | Chickpea salad, quinoa, balsamic dressing | Baked cod (3.5 oz), sweet potato, green beans |
| Wednesday | Oatmeal, apple, cinnamon | Vegetable bean stew, cornbread (no fat) | Stir-fried vegetables (water-sauté) over buckwheat noodles |
| Thursday | Whole grain pancakes, fruit | Split pea soup, whole grain bread, salad | Lentil curry, brown rice, roasted cauliflower |
| Thursday | Whole grain pancakes, fruit | Split pea soup, whole grain bread, salad | Lentil curry, brown rice, roasted cauliflower |
| Friday | Oatmeal, berries, walnuts (0) | 10-grain salad with chickpeas, lemon dressing | Baked potato (no butter), vegetarian chili, side salad |
| Saturday | Fruit smoothie (fruit + ice, no dairy) | Vegetable minestrone, whole grain crackers | Skinless chicken breast (3.5 oz), roasted vegetables, quinoa |
| Sunday | Whole grain waffles, fruit compote | Bean and corn salad, brown rice | Tofu stir-fry (no oil), brown rice, bok choy |

> **Cooking technique note:** All cooking must be done without added fat. Vegetables are steamed, boiled, roasted (with water/broth to prevent sticking), or sautéed using the "water sauté" technique (deglazing with small amounts of water or broth instead of oil).

## Pritikin Calorie Density Guide

The Pritikin Principle (Robert Pritikin's refinement) focuses on calorie density — the number of calories per gram of food. Lower calorie density foods allow you to eat more volume while consuming fewer calories:

| Calorie Density (cal/g) | Category | Examples |
|:------------------------:|----------|----------|
| 0–0.6 | **Very Low** — eat freely | Most vegetables, broth-based soups, fruits |
| 0.6–1.5 | **Low** — eat generously | Whole grains (cooked), potatoes, beans, lentils, corn |
| 1.5–3.0 | **Moderate** — eat with awareness | Bread, dried fruit, pasta, lean fish/poultry |
| 3.0–9.0 | **High** — minimize or avoid | Cheese, meat, nuts, seeds |
| 9.0+ | **Very High** — avoid entirely | Oils, butter, fats |

> **Citation:** Rolls BJ, Bell EA, Thorwart M. "Water incorporated into a food but not served with a food decreases energy intake in lean women." *American Journal of Clinical Nutrition*. 1999;70(4):448-455.
> **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1093/ajcn/70.4.448

## Key Nutritional Considerations

### Nutrients Adequately Provided
- **Complex carbohydrates:** Very high — whole grains, vegetables, fruits, legumes
- **Fiber:** 35–45g/day — among the highest of any diet
- **Potassium:** Very high from fruits and vegetables — supports blood pressure
- **Antioxidants/polyphenols:** Very high from abundant plant foods
- **Vitamin C, folate, magnesium:** Abundantly provided

### Nutrients to Monitor
| Nutrient | Risk Level | Concern | Solution |
|----------|:---------:|---------|----------|
| **Vitamin B12** | High | No animal foods (or very limited) | Supplement; fortified foods |
| **Vitamin D** | Moderate-High | No fatty fish, no dairy, no egg yolks | Fortified plant milks, sunlight, supplement |
| **Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)** | Moderate-High | No fatty fish, no nuts/seeds (except small flaxseed) | Ground flaxseed (1 tbsp/day), algae-based DHA supplement |
| **Calcium** | Moderate | No dairy (or very limited) | Fortified plant milks, leafy greens, tofu (calcium-set) |
| **Iron** | Moderate | Non-heme iron from plants is less bioavailable | Pair with vitamin C; use cast iron cookware |
| **Zinc** | Moderate | Reduced from eliminated animal foods | Whole grains, legumes, seeds (small amounts) |
| **Fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K)** | Moderate | Very low fat reduces absorption | Include traces of fat naturally present in food; consider supplement |
