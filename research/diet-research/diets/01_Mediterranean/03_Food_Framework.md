# Mediterranean Diet — Food Framework

> Practical guide to what to eat, avoid, and limit on the Mediterranean diet, including macronutrient targets, portion guidelines, and sample meal structures.

---

## Macronutrient Targets

| Macronutrient | Target (% of total calories) | Notes |
|---------------|:----------------------------:|-------|
| **Carbohydrates** | 40–50% | Primarily from whole grains, legumes, vegetables, fruits |
| **Protein** | 15–20% | From fish, legumes, nuts, poultry, eggs, dairy |
| **Fat** | 30–40% | Primarily monounsaturated (olive oil); ~15-20% MUFA, <8% SFA |
| **Saturated fat** | <8% | Minimize |
| **Fiber** | 25–35 g/day | From plant foods |
| **Sodium** | <2,300 mg/day | Naturally moderate; herbs/spices replace salt |

> The Mediterranean diet is moderate in all macronutrients — it is neither high-carb nor low-carb, neither high-fat nor low-fat. The key distinction is the *quality* of each macronutrient.

## Foods to Eat Daily (The Base)

### Vegetables (4+ servings/day)
Eat a wide variety, focusing on color diversity:

| Food | Examples | Serving Size |
|------|----------|:------------:|
| Leafy greens | Spinach, kale, arugula, Swiss chard, romaine | 1 cup raw / ½ cup cooked |
| Tomatoes | Fresh, canned (no added sugar), tomato sauce | 1 medium / ½ cup |
| Alliums | Onions, garlic, leeks, shallots | 1 clove garlic / ½ onion |
| Cruciferous | Broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage, Brussels sprouts | ½ cup cooked |
| Peppers | Bell peppers (red, yellow, green), chili peppers | 1 medium |
| Root vegetables | Carrots, beets, sweet potatoes (moderate) | ½ cup cooked |
| Eggplant, zucchini, cucumber | Core Mediterranean vegetables | ½ cup cooked |
| Artichokes | Fresh or jarred (in water, not oil) | 1 medium |

### Fruits (3+ servings/day)
| Food | Examples | Serving Size |
|------|----------|:------------:|
| Berries | Strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, figs | 1 cup |
| Citrus | Oranges, lemons, grapefruit | 1 medium |
| Stone fruit | Peaches, plums, apricots, cherries | 1 medium |
| Grapes, pomegranate | Fresh only | 1 cup / ½ cup |
| Apples, pears | Fresh, with skin | 1 medium |
| Melons | Watermelon, cantaloupe | 1 cup |

### Whole Grains (3–6 servings/day)
| Food | Examples | Serving Size |
|------|----------|:------------:|
| Whole grain bread | Sourdough, whole wheat, pita | 1 slice |
| Pasta | Whole wheat pasta (al dente, moderate portions) | ½–1 cup cooked |
| Rice | Brown rice, wild rice | ½ cup cooked |
| Other grains | Quinoa, farro, bulgur, couscous, barley, oats | ½ cup cooked |
| Polenta, cornmeal | Stone-ground | ½ cup cooked |

### Extra Virgin Olive Oil (4+ tablespoons/day)
- The **primary fat source** — used for cooking, dressing, finishing
- Choose **extra virgin** (cold-pressed, highest polyphenol content)
- Approx. 1 tbsp = 120 calories, so total intake ~480 calories from olive oil

### Legumes & Beans (3+ servings/week, ideally more)
| Food | Serving Size |
|------|:------------:|
| Lentils (all varieties) | ½ cup cooked |
| Chickpeas (garbanzo beans) | ½ cup cooked |
| White beans (cannellini, navy) | ½ cup cooked |
| Black beans, kidney beans | ½ cup cooked |
| Hummus | ¼ cup |
| Split peas (for soup) | ½ cup cooked |

### Nuts & Seeds (1+ handful/day, ~30g)
| Food | Serving Size |
|------|:------------:|
| Almonds, walnuts, pistachios | ~30g (small handful) |
| Pine nuts, hazelnuts, cashews | ~30g |
| Flaxseeds, chia seeds | 1–2 tbsp |
| Sesame seeds, tahini | 1–2 tbsp |
| Pumpkin seeds, sunflower seeds | ~30g |

### Herbs & Spices (liberal use)
- Replace salt with: garlic, basil, oregano, rosemary, thyme, sage, parsley, mint
- Add: turmeric, cinnamon, cumin, coriander, saffron, paprika, black pepper
- Fresh lemon juice as a primary flavoring

### Water (primary beverage)
- Hydration from water and herbal teas
- Coffee and tea are acceptable (unsweetened)

## Foods to Eat Frequently (2+ times/week)

### Fish & Seafood (at least 2 servings/week — ideally 3+)
| Food | Omega-3 Content | Serving Size |
|------|:---------------:|:------------:|
| Sardines, anchovies | ★★★ Very high | 85–140g |
| Salmon, mackerel, herring | ★★★ Very high | 85–140g |
| Tuna (fresh or canned) | ★★☆ High | 85–140g |
| Trout, sea bass, sea bream | ★★☆ High | 85–140g |
| Shrimp, mussels, clams, squid | ★☆☆ Moderate | 85–140g |
| Cod, halibut, snapper | ★☆☆ Moderate | 85–140g |

> **PREDIMED recommendation:** At least 3 servings/week of fish, prioritizing fatty fish rich in EPA/DHA.

### Poultry (moderate, weekly)
- Chicken, turkey — preferably free-range
- Serving size: 85–120g
- Prepared grilled, roasted, or braised (not fried)

### Eggs (4 per week)
- Any preparation, but poached or soft-boiled is traditional
- Include in vegetable frittatas, shakshuka

## Foods to Eat in Moderation (Daily, small amounts)

### Dairy
| Food | Serving Size | Frequency |
|------|:------------:|-----------|
| Greek yogurt (plain, full-fat) | 1 cup | Daily |
| Cheese (feta, goat, manchego, parmesan) | 30–40g | Daily, small portions |
| Ricotta, mozzarella, halloumi | 30–40g | A few times/week |

### Olives
| Food | Serving Size | Frequency |
|------|:------------:|-----------|
| Kalamata, green olives | 5–10 olives | Daily or several times/week |

### Wine (optional, with meals)
| Population | Amount |
|------------|--------|
| Women | 1 glass/day (120–150ml) |
| Men | 1–2 glasses/day (120–150ml each) |
| Non-drinkers | **Do not start drinking for health reasons** |
| Pregnant women | Avoid |

> **Caveat:** Recent evidence (including WHO guidance) suggests that no level of alcohol consumption is completely safe for cancer risk. Wine is optional and should only be consumed by those who already drink moderately.

## Foods to Limit / Minimize

| Food | Reason |
|------|--------|
| **Red meat** (beef, pork, lamb) | A few times per month max; keep portions small (85–120g) |
| **Processed meats** (sausages, bacon, deli meats) | Rarely or avoid — WHO classifies as Group 1 carcinogen |
| **Refined grains** (white bread, white rice, regular pasta) | Replace with whole grain versions |
| **Refined sugar & sweets** | Occasional treats only; fruit is the typical dessert |
| **Sugary drinks** (soda, fruit juice with added sugar) | Avoid |
| **Fast food & ultra-processed foods** | Avoid |
| **Butter & margarine** | Replace with olive oil |
| **Salt** | Minimize; use herbs/spices/lemon instead |

## Daily Eating Pattern (Sample Day)

### Breakfast
- Whole grain sourdough toast with mashed avocado and tomato
- Drizzle of olive oil, sprinkle of feta
- Coffee or herbal tea
- *Or:* Greek yogurt with walnuts, honey, and berries

### Lunch
- Large salad: mixed greens, tomato, cucumber, olives, chickpeas
- Dressing: olive oil + lemon juice + oregano
- Grilled fish or chicken (if non-vegetarian)
- Slice of whole grain bread

### Snack (optional)
- Handful of almonds or walnuts
- Fresh fruit (apple, pear, or orange)
- *Or:* hummus with vegetables

### Dinner
- Baked salmon or lentil stew
- Roasted vegetables (eggplant, zucchini, peppers, tomatoes)
- Small portion of whole grain pasta or quinoa
- Glass of red wine (optional)

### Dessert (optional)
- Fresh fruit
- *Or occasionally:* small piece of dark chocolate or a traditional pastry

## Weekly Meal Structure (Traditional Mediterranean)

| Day | Lunch | Dinner |
|-----|-------|--------|
| Monday | Lentil soup with bread and salad | Grilled fish with roasted vegetables |
| Tuesday | Chickpea and spinach stew | Chicken with lemon and herbs, salad |
| Wednesday | Pasta with tomato sauce and vegetables | Bean and vegetable soup |
| Thursday | Greek salad with feta and olives | Grilled sardines, bread, tomatoes |
| Friday | Fish with vegetables and rice | Vegetable frittata with bread and salad |
| Saturday | Lamb (small portion) with vegetables | Pasta with seafood |
| Sunday | Big family meal: fish or legume dish | Leftovers or light soup |

> **Note:** The traditional Mediterranean pattern features the **main meal at lunch** and a lighter dinner — the opposite of most Western eating patterns. This timing may contribute to metabolic benefits.

## The Mediterranean Diet Adherence Score (14-Point MEDAS)

The validated tool used in PREDIMED and most Mediterranean diet research:

| Question | Criterion for 1 Point |
|----------|----------------------|
| 1. Olive oil as main culinary fat? | Yes |
| 2. ≥4 tbsp olive oil/day? | Yes |
| 3. ≥2 servings vegetables/day? | Yes |
| 4. ≥2 fruit servings/day? | Yes |
| 5. <1 serving red meat/day? | Yes |
| 6. <1 serving butter/day? | Yes |
| 7. <1 sweet/drink with sugar/day? | Yes |
| 8. ≥3 servings legumes/week? | Yes |
| 9. ≥3 servings fish/week? | Yes |
| 10. <2 commercial pastries/week? | Yes |
| 11. ≥1 serving nuts/week? | Yes |
| 12. ≥3 poultry vs. red meat? | More poultry |
| 13. ≥2 vegetable-based sauces/week (sofrito/tomato)? | Yes |
| 14. ≥7 glasses wine/week? | Yes (optional) |

> **Score ≥9** = good Mediterranean diet adherence (PREDIMED criterion)
> **Score ≥11** = high adherence

> **Citation:** Schröder H, Fitó M, Estruch R, et al. "A short screener is valid for assessing Mediterranean diet adherence among older Spanish men and women." *Journal of Nutrition*. 2011;141(6):1140-1145.
> **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.3945/jn.110.135566

## Key Nutritional Considerations

### Nutrients Adequately Provided
- **Healthy fats:** Monounsaturated (olive oil), omega-3 (fish, walnuts, flaxseed)
- **Fiber:** 25–35g/day from whole grains, legumes, vegetables, fruits
- **Antioxidants/polyphenols:** Among the highest of any dietary pattern
- **Potassium:** High from vegetables/fruits — supports blood pressure
- **Magnesium:** From nuts, seeds, whole grains, leafy greens
- **Folate:** From legumes, leafy greens
- **Vitamin C, E, K:** From vegetables, fruits, olive oil

### Nutrients to Monitor
- **Vitamin B12:** Adequate from fish, dairy, eggs. Vegetarians need to ensure intake.
- **Iron:** Non-heme iron from plant sources is less bioavailable. Enhance with vitamin C.
- **Calcium:** Adequate from dairy, but vegans within the pattern need alternatives.
- **Vitamin D:** May be insufficient without fatty fish or sun exposure.
- **Iodine:** Ensure adequate from fish/seafood.
