# High-Protein Diet — Food Framework

> Practical guide to achieving high-protein intake, including macronutrient targets, protein-rich food sources with serving sizes, sample meal plans, and strategies for distributing protein across the day.

---

## Macronutrient Targets

| Macronutrient | Target (% of total calories) | Notes |
|---------------|:----------------------------:|-------|
| **Protein** | 25–35% | Minimum: 1.6 g/kg body weight/day; Athletic: 1.6–2.2 g/kg; Very aggressive deficit: up to 2.5–3.0 g/kg |
| **Carbohydrates** | 30–45% | Adjust based on activity level and chosen food diet; prioritize whole-food sources |
| **Fat** | 25–35% | Include healthy fats (olive oil, nuts, avocado); do not eliminate fat |
| **Saturated fat** | <10% | Choose lean protein sources to keep saturated fat moderate |
| **Fiber** | 25–35 g/day | Critical — high-protein diets can be low in fiber if relying on animal protein exclusively |
| **Sodium** | <2,300 mg/day | Moderate; be mindful of sodium in processed protein sources (deli meat, canned tuna) |

### Protein Targets by Goal

| Goal | Protein Target (g/kg body weight) | For a 70 kg (154 lb) person | Notes |
|------|:---------------------------------:|:---------------------------:|-------|
| RDA (prevent deficiency) | 0.8 g/kg | 56 g/day | Minimum to prevent nitrogen imbalance |
| General health optimization | 1.0–1.2 g/kg | 70–84 g/day | Slightly above RDA; good for sedentary adults |
| Weight loss (satiety + preservation) | 1.2–1.6 g/kg | 84–112 g/day | Targets the satiety and lean-mass benefits |
| Active adult / fitness | 1.6–2.0 g/kg | 112–140 g/day | For regular exercisers |
| Muscle building (hypertrophy) | 1.6–2.2 g/kg | 112–154 g/day | Maximum benefit per Morton et al. 2018 |
| Aggressive caloric deficit (cutting) | 2.2–3.0 g/kg | 154–210 g/day | Maximizes lean mass preservation during deficit |
| Older adults (65+, sarcopenia prevention) | 1.0–1.2 g/kg | 70–84 g/day | Per PROT-AGE recommendations; higher per meal (30–40g) to overcome anabolic resistance |
| Endurance athlete | 1.2–1.6 g/kg | 84–112 g/day | Per ACSM/ISSN |

### The Per-Meal Protein Distribution Rule

To maximize muscle protein synthesis and satiety, distribute protein across meals:

| Meal | Protein Target | Rationale |
|------|:--------------:|----------|
| **Breakfast** | 25–40g | Often the lowest-protein meal; critical for morning satiety (Leidy et al.) |
| **Lunch** | 30–40g | Second protein dose |
| **Snack (optional)** | 10–20g | Bridges gap between meals; protein shake or Greek yogurt |
| **Dinner** | 30–50g | Final protein dose; casein-rich sources (cottage cheese, dairy) for overnight release |
| **Pre-sleep (optional)** | 30–40g casein | Slow-release protein for overnight MPS |

> **Leucine threshold:** Each main meal should contain **≥2.5–3g leucine** (equivalent to ~25–30g of high-quality animal protein or ~35–40g of plant protein) to maximally stimulate muscle protein synthesis.

## Foods to Limit / Minimize

| Food | Reason |
|------|--------|
| **Processed meats** (sausage, bacon, hot dogs, deli meats) | WHO Group 1 carcinogen; high in sodium, saturated fat, preservatives. Choose fresh lean protein instead |
| **High-fat cuts of red meat** (ribeye, T-bone, brisket) | High in saturated fat; lean cuts (sirloin, flank) are fine in moderation |
| **Fried protein** (fried chicken, fish & chips) | Adds empty calories from frying oil; negates leanness of the protein |
| **Ultra-processed protein bars with high sugar** | Some protein bars are glorified candy bars (>20g sugar); choose low-sugar options |
| **Sugary protein smoothies** | Commercial smoothies can have 500+ calories and 50g+ sugar; make your own |
| **Full-fat cheese in large amounts** | Calorie-dense; small amounts are fine but large amounts displace lean protein |
| **Processed cheese products** | Low-quality protein; high in sodium and additives |

## Daily Eating Pattern (Sample Day)

### Sample Day: 1,800 calories, 140g protein (78kg person, 1.8 g/kg)

#### Breakfast (8:00 AM) — 40g protein
- Greek yogurt parfait: 1 cup non-fat Greek yogurt (20g protein) + ½ cup berries + 1 oz almonds + 1 tbsp chia seeds
- 2 scrambled eggs with spinach (12g protein)
- 1 slice whole grain toast
- *Total: ~450 calories, 38g protein*

#### Snack (11:00 AM) — 15g protein
- 1 cup cottage cheese (low-fat) with cherry tomatoes
- *Or:* 1 scoop whey protein shake with water or unsweetened almond milk
- *Total: ~120 calories, 15g protein*

#### Lunch (1:00 PM) — 40g protein
- Grilled chicken breast (150g / 46g protein)
- Large mixed salad with olive oil dressing
- ½ cup quinoa
- *Total: ~450 calories, 42g protein*

#### Snack (4:00 PM) — 20g protein
- Protein shake: 1 scoop whey protein + water + 1 banana
- *Or:* 2 hard-boiled eggs + apple
- *Total: ~200 calories, 20g protein*

#### Dinner (7:00 PM) — 45g protein
- Baked salmon (170g / 42g protein)
- Roasted broccoli and sweet potato
- *Total: ~580 calories, 45g protein*

**Daily total: ~1,800 calories, 160g protein (2.0 g/kg for 78kg)**

## Protein Distribution Strategies

### The "30-30-30" Method
The simplest high-protein distribution: **~30g protein per meal, 3 meals per day** = ~90g total.

| Meal | Protein | Example |
|------|:-------:|---------|
| Breakfast | 30g | 1 cup Greek yogurt (20g) + 2 eggs (12g) |
| Lunch | 30g | 120g chicken breast (37g) — slightly above target |
| Dinner | 30g | 120g salmon (30g) |

### The "40-40-40" Method (for active individuals)
~120g total, suitable for someone weighing 65–75 kg aiming for 1.6–2.0 g/kg.

| Meal | Protein | Example |
|------|:-------:|---------|
| Breakfast | 40g | Protein shake (36g whey) + 1 cup milk (8g) |
| Lunch | 40g | 140g chicken breast (43g) |
| Dinner | 40g | 170g salmon (42g) |

### The Snack Strategy (4 meals)
Adding a protein-rich snack between meals helps reach higher totals and maintains MPS stimulation.

| Meal | Protein |
|------|:-------:|
| Breakfast | 35g |
| Snack | 15g |
| Lunch | 35g |
| Snack | 15g |
| Dinner | 40g |
| **Total** | **140g** |

## Key Nutritional Considerations

### Nutrients Adequately Provided
- **Complete amino acids:** Animal protein sources provide all essential amino acids in optimal ratios
- **B12 (cobalamin):** Abundant from animal protein (meat, fish, eggs, dairy)
- **Iron (heme):** Highly bioavailable from red meat; less so from poultry/fish
- **Zinc:** Rich in red meat, poultry, pumpkin seeds
- **Calcium:** From dairy (Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, milk)
- **Creatine:** Found naturally in red meat and fish; supports muscle energy and performance
- **Leucine:** Key amino acid for MPS; highest in whey, dairy, meat, poultry

### Nutrients to Monitor
- **Fiber:** Animal protein contains zero fiber. A high-protein diet MUST include generous vegetables, legumes, fruits, and whole grains. Target 25–35g/day minimum.
- **Folate:** Lower in high-meat diets; ensure leafy greens, legumes, fortified grains
- **Vitamin C:** Include fruits and vegetables for antioxidant balance
- **Phytochemicals/antioxidants:** Diverse vegetable and fruit intake is essential alongside protein-rich animal foods
- **Omega-3 fatty acids:** Ensure fatty fish (salmon, sardines) 2–3x/week or supplement
- **Calcium (if dairy-free):** Fortified plant milks, leafy greens, sardines with bones

| Lentils/dry beans | ~$0.30 |
| Whey protein (bulk) | ~$0.80–$1.50 |
| Peanut butter | ~$0.40 |

### High-Protein While Traveling or Eating Out
- **Restaurants:** Order a double protein portion, or add a side of grilled chicken/fish
- **Fast food:** Grilled chicken sandwiches (no mayo), burger patties (no bun if low-carb), side salads with grilled chicken
- **Convenience stores:** Hard-boiled eggs, Greek yogurt, jerky (low-sugar), protein bars, string cheese
- **Hotels:** Protein powder in a shaker bottle (just add water)

### High-Protein + Intermittent Fasting
- Excellent combination for body composition
- Within the eating window, each meal must contain more protein (30–50g per meal) to compensate for fewer meals
- Prioritize fast-digesting protein (whey) right after breaking the fast
- Include casein (cottage cheese) at the last meal for overnight release
