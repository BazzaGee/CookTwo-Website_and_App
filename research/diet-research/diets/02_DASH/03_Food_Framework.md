# DASH Diet — Food Framework

> Practical guide to what to eat, avoid, and limit on the DASH diet, including macronutrient targets, serving guidelines, and sample meal structures. Based on the NHLBI's official DASH eating plan (NIH Publication No. 06-4082).

---

## Macronutrient Targets

| Macronutrient | Target (% of total calories) | Notes |
|---------------|:----------------------------:|-------|
| **Carbohydrates** | ~55% | Primarily from whole grains, fruits, vegetables, legumes |
| **Protein** | ~18% | From poultry, fish, dairy, legumes, nuts |
| **Total fat** | ~27% | Reduced from typical American diet (~35%) |
| **Saturated fat** | ≤6% | Very low — minimize animal fats, tropical oils |
| **Cholesterol** | ≤150 mg/day | |
| **Fiber** | ~30 g/day | From whole grains, fruits, vegetables, legumes |
| **Sodium** | ≤2,300 mg/day (target: ≤1,500 mg/day) | The defining feature of DASH |
| **Potassium** | 4,700 mg/day | From food, not supplements |
| **Calcium** | 1,250 mg/day | From low-fat dairy, leafy greens, fortified foods |
| **Magnesium** | 500 mg/day | From whole grains, nuts, seeds, vegetables |

> The DASH diet is moderate in all macronutrients but is distinguished by its **low sodium, high potassium/magnesium/calcium, and low saturated fat** profile. It is not a low-carb, low-fat, or high-protein diet — it is a **whole-food, nutrient-dense, mineral-rich** diet.

## Foods to Eat Daily (The Base)

### Vegetables (4–5 servings/day)

The cornerstone of DASH. Aim for variety and color diversity.

| Food | Examples | Serving Size |
|------|----------|:------------:|
| Leafy greens | Spinach, kale, collard greens, Swiss chard, romaine | 1 cup raw / ½ cup cooked |
| Tomatoes | Fresh, canned (no salt added), low-sodium tomato sauce | 1 medium / ½ cup |
| Cruciferous | Broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage, Brussels sprouts | ½ cup cooked |
| Root vegetables | Carrots, beets, sweet potatoes | ½ cup cooked |
| Peppers | Bell peppers (red, yellow, green) | 1 medium |
| Alliums | Onions, garlic, leeks, shallots | ½ cup / 1 clove garlic |
| Squash/cucumbers | Zucchini, yellow squash, cucumber, eggplant | ½ cup cooked |
| Green beans, peas | Fresh or frozen (not canned with salt) | ½ cup cooked |
| Canned vegetables | Only if "no salt added" or rinsed thoroughly | ½ cup |

> **Sodium tip:** If using canned vegetables, choose "no salt added" varieties or rinse thoroughly under cold water to remove up to 40% of sodium.

### Fruits (4–5 servings/day)

| Food | Examples | Serving Size |
|------|----------|:------------:|
| Citrus | Oranges, grapefruit, tangerines, lemons | 1 medium |
| Berries | Strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, blackberries | 1 cup |
| Apples, pears | Fresh, with skin | 1 medium |
| Bananas | Particularly high in potassium | 1 medium |
| Stone fruit | Peaches, plums, nectarines, apricots | 1 medium |
| Melons | Watermelon, cantaloupe, honeydew | 1 cup |
| Grapes, cherries | Fresh | 1 cup / 15 cherries |
| Dried fruit | Apricots, raisins, prunes (small portions — calorie-dense) | ¼ cup |

> **Potassium highlight:** Bananas, oranges, cantaloupe, prunes, and raisins are especially high in potassium — a key mineral in DASH's BP-lowering mechanism.

### Low-Fat or Fat-Free Dairy (2–3 servings/day)

Critical for calcium and the BP-lowering effect (the original DASH trial showed dairy was the component that distinguished DASH from the fruits-and-vegetables-only arm).

| Food | Serving Size |
|------|:------------:|
| Fat-free or 1% milk | 1 cup |
| Fat-free or low-fat yogurt (plain) | 1 cup |
| Low-fat cheese (part-skim mozzarella, reduced-fat cheddar) | 1.5 oz |
| Fat-free cottage cheese | ½ cup |
| Low-fat buttermilk | 1 cup |

> **Lactose intolerant?** Use lactose-free dairy or fortified plant milks (soy, almond — choose unsweetened, calcium-fortified). Ensure you still meet calcium targets.

### Whole Grains (6–8 servings/day)

| Food | Examples | Serving Size |
|------|----------|:------------:|
| Whole grain bread | 100% whole wheat, rye, pumpernickel | 1 slice |
| Brown rice, wild rice | | ½ cup cooked |
| Oats | Steel-cut, rolled (not instant with added sugar) | ½ cup cooked |
| Quinoa | | ½ cup cooked |
| Whole wheat pasta | | ½ cup cooked |
| Barley, bulgur, farro | | ½ cup cooked |
| Whole grain cereal | Low-sugar, high-fiber (e.g., bran flakes, shredded wheat) | 1 oz / ¾–1 cup |
| Whole grain crackers | Low-sodium varieties | 5–8 crackers |
| Corn tortillas (whole grain) | | 1 small tortilla |

> **Sodium tip:** Bread is a major hidden sodium source. Check labels — aim for ≤150 mg sodium per slice.

### Lean Proteins (≤6 servings/day combined)

| Food | Serving Size |
|------|:------------:|
| Skinless chicken breast | 3 oz (cooked) |
| Skinless turkey | 3 oz |
| Fish (all types — fatty fish especially) | 3 oz |
| Eggs | 1 large (limit yolks — 4/week per DASH) |
| Egg whites | 2 whites = 1 serving |

> **Sodium tip:** Avoid deli meats, which are extremely high in sodium (even "reduced sodium" versions). Cook fresh poultry instead.

## Foods to Eat Frequently (Weekly)

### Nuts, Seeds, and Legumes (4–5 servings/week)

| Food | Serving Size |
|------|:------------:|
| Almonds, walnuts, pistachios, mixed nuts (unsalted) | 1.5 oz (~⅓ cup) |
| Sunflower seeds, pumpkin seeds (unsalted) | 2 tbsp |
| Peanut butter (natural, no salt added) | 2 tbsp |
| Lentils, split peas | ½ cup cooked |
| Chickpeas, black beans, kidney beans (dried or low-sodium canned) | ½ cup cooked |
| Flaxseeds, chia seeds | 1 tbsp |

> Nuts and seeds are excellent sources of magnesium and healthy fats. Always choose **unsalted** varieties.

### Fish (2–3 servings/week)

| Food | Omega-3 Content | Serving Size |
|------|:---------------:|:------------:|
| Salmon, mackerel, herring | ★★★ Very high | 3 oz |
| Sardines, anchovies (unsalted or low-sodium) | ★★★ Very high | 3 oz |
| Tuna (fresh or canned, low-sodium) | ★★☆ High | 3 oz |
| Trout, halibut | ★★☆ High | 3 oz |
| Cod, tilapia, flounder | ★☆☆ Moderate | 3 oz |

## Foods to Eat in Moderation

### Fats and Oils (2–3 servings/day)

| Food | Serving Size |
|------|:------------:|
| Olive oil, canola oil | 1 tsp |
| Soft tub margarine (no trans fats) | 1 tsp |
| Light mayonnaise | 1 tsp |
| Avocado | ¼ medium |

### Sweets (≤5 servings/week)

| Food | Serving Size |
|------|:------------:|
| Fruit gelatin, sorbet | ½ cup |
| Maple syrup, honey | 1 tbsp |
| Jelly, jam | ½ tbsp |
| Hard candy | 1 piece |
| Low-fat granola bar | 1 small |

> DASH allows small amounts of sweets but emphasizes that added sugars should be minimized. The default dessert is fruit.

## Foods to Limit / Minimize

| Food | Reason |
|------|--------|
| **High-sodium foods** (restaurant meals, canned soups, frozen dinners, deli meats) | **The #1 target to reduce** — these account for 75%+ of sodium in the American diet |
| **Salt** (table salt, sea salt, kosher salt — all are sodium chloride) | Use herbs, spices, citrus, and vinegar instead |
| **Processed meats** (sausage, bacon, hot dogs, salami, ham) | Extremely high in sodium + nitrates + saturated fat |
| **Red meat** (beef, pork, lamb) | Higher in saturated fat — limit to small portions occasionally |
| **Full-fat dairy** (whole milk, cream, regular cheese, butter) | High in saturated fat |
| **Fried foods** | High in saturated/trans fats and sodium |
| **Refined grains** (white bread, white rice, regular pasta) | Replace with whole grain versions |
| **Sugary drinks** (soda, juice drinks, sweetened coffee) | Empty calories; linked to hypertension |
| **Salty snacks** (potato chips, pretzels, crackers, salted nuts) | Very high in sodium |
| **Soy sauce and condiments** | Extremely high in sodium; use low-sodium alternatives sparingly |

## The Sodium Challenge: How to Hit 1,500 mg/day

The biggest practical challenge of DASH is sodium restriction. Here's how the sodium budget breaks down:

| Source | Typical American | DASH Target |
|--------|:----------------:|:-----------:|
| Processed/restaurant foods | 75% | <30% |
| Home cooking (salt added) | 10–15% | 5% |
| Naturally occurring in food | 10–15% | 60–70% |

### Practical Strategies
1. **Cook from scratch** — avoid restaurant meals and ultra-processed foods
2. **Read labels** — look for "no salt added," "low sodium" (≤140 mg/serving), or "reduced sodium"
3. **Flavor without salt** — use herbs, spices, citrus juice, vinegar, garlic, onion, and no-salt blends
4. **Rinse canned foods** — removes up to 40% of sodium
5. **Watch "hidden" sodium** — bread, cereal, condiments, and even some medications contain sodium

## Daily Eating Pattern (Sample Day — ~2,100 calories, 1,500 mg sodium)

### Breakfast
- ¾ cup steel-cut oats with 1 medium banana (sliced) and 2 tbsp walnuts
- 1 cup fat-free milk or yogurt
- 1 slice whole wheat toast with 1 tsp soft tub margarine
- Coffee or tea (unsweetened)

### Lunch
- Large spinach salad: 2 cups fresh spinach, ½ cup cherry tomatoes, ½ cup cucumber, ¼ cup chickpeas, 2 tbsp unsalted sunflower seeds
- Dressing: 1 tbsp olive oil + lemon juice + herbs
- 3 oz grilled chicken breast (unseasoned or herb-seasoned, no salt)
- 1 orange
- Water or sparkling water

### Snack (optional)
- 1 medium apple
- 1 oz unsalted almonds (~23 nuts)
- *Or:* ½ cup carrot and celery sticks with 2 tbsp hummus (low-sodium)

### Dinner
- 3 oz baked salmon with lemon and dill (no added salt)
- ½ cup cooked brown rice
- ½ cup steamed broccoli with garlic (no salt)
- ½ cup roasted sweet potato wedges
- Side salad with mixed greens and oil-free vinaigrette
- Water or sparkling water with lime

### Dessert (optional)
- ½ cup fresh berries
- *Or occasionally:* ½ cup fruit sorbet

## Weekly Meal Structure (DASH-Style)

| Day | Lunch | Dinner |
|-----|-------|--------|
| Monday | Lentil and vegetable soup, whole grain bread, side salad | Grilled chicken breast with roasted vegetables and brown rice |
| Tuesday | Turkey sandwich on whole wheat bread (no deli meat — cook fresh turkey), apple | Baked cod with quinoa and steamed green beans |
| Wednesday | Chickpea salad with cucumber, tomatoes, and olive oil-lemon dressing | Grilled salmon with sweet potato and broccoli |
| Thursday | Spinach and black bean salad, orange, handful of unsalted walnuts | Vegetable stir-fry with tofu and brown rice (low-sodium soy sauce alternative) |
| Friday | Tuna (low-sodium canned) on whole grain crackers, carrot sticks | Grilled chicken with roasted root vegetables and barley |
| Saturday | Lentil and vegetable stew, whole grain bread | Baked turkey (fresh, not deli) with quinoa and Brussels sprouts |
| Sunday | Large mixed salad with hard-boiled egg and sunflower seeds, banana | Fish tacos with cabbage slaw (no-salt seasoning) on corn tortillas |

> **Note:** Each day includes 4–5 servings of vegetables, 4–5 servings of fruit, 2–3 servings of low-fat dairy (not all shown above — add milk/yogurt with breakfast and snacks), and 6–8 servings of whole grains across all meals.

## Key Nutritional Considerations

### Nutrients Adequately Provided
- **Potassium:** ~4,700 mg/day from fruits, vegetables, dairy (far exceeds typical American intake of ~2,500–3,000 mg)
- **Calcium:** ~1,250 mg/day from low-fat dairy (meets RDA for most adults)
- **Magnesium:** ~500 mg/day from whole grains, nuts, vegetables
- **Fiber:** ~30 g/day from whole grains, fruits, vegetables, legumes
- **Antioxidants:** High from 8–10 produce servings/day
- **Folate, vitamin C, vitamin K:** Abundant from vegetables

### Nutrients to Monitor
- **Vitamin D:** Low-fat dairy provides some; fatty fish provides more. May need supplementation.
- **Iron:** Lean meat/poultry/fish provide heme iron. If minimizing red meat, ensure adequate iron from other sources.
- **B12:** Adequate from animal proteins. Vegans modifying DASH need supplementation.
- **Omega-3 (EPA/DHA):** Aim for 2+ servings of fatty fish per week to meet needs.
