# Paleo Diet — Food Framework

> Practical guide to what to eat, avoid, and limit on the Paleo diet, including macronutrient targets, portion guidelines, and sample meal structures.

---

## Macronutrient Targets

| Macronutrient | Target (% of total calories) | Notes |
|---------------|:----------------------------:|-------|
| **Carbohydrates** | 20–30% | From fruits, vegetables, nuts; no grains or legumes |
| **Protein** | 25–35% | High; from meat, fish, eggs |
| **Fat** | 35–45% | From olive oil, nuts, seeds, avocado, animal fat, coconut oil |
| **Saturated fat** | 10–15% | Variable; higher in Primal versions, lower in strict Cordain |
| **Fiber** | 25–40 g/day | From vegetables, fruits, nuts, seeds (no grain fiber) |
| **Sodium** | <1,500–2,300 mg/day | No added salt in strict versions; moderate in practice |

> The Paleo diet is moderate-to-low in carbohydrate, high in protein, and moderate-to-high in fat. The exact ratios vary significantly between strict Cordain Paleo (lower fat, leaner meats) and Primal/modified versions (higher fat, fattier meats). Macronutrient ratios in published studies typically range from **~20-30% carb / ~25-30% protein / ~35-45% fat**.

## Foods to Eat Daily (The Core)

### Meat & Poultry (1–2 servings/day)

| Food | Examples | Serving Size |
|------|----------|:------------:|
| Grass-fed beef | Steak, ground beef, roasts | 120–170g |
| Bison, venison, wild game | Leaner than conventional beef | 120–170g |
| Lamb | Chops, ground, roasts | 120–170g |
| Pork | Pasture-raised preferred | 120–170g |
| Chicken, turkey | Thighs, breasts, whole bird (skinless in strict) | 120–170g |
| Organ meats | Liver, heart, kidney (nutrient-dense) | 60–120g |

> **Preference order:** Grass-fed > pasture-raised > conventional. Wild game is ideal. Organ meats are encouraged for micronutrient density (especially liver for vitamin A, B12, folate, iron).

### Fish & Seafood (1–2 servings/day, or at least 3+/week)

| Food | Omega-3 Content | Serving Size |
|------|:---------------:|:------------:|
| Wild salmon | ★★★ Very high | 120–170g |
| Sardines, anchovies | ★★★ Very high | 60–120g |
| Mackerel, herring | ★★★ Very high | 120–170g |
| Trout, Arctic char | ★★☆ High | 120–170g |
| Tuna (fresh/canned) | ★★☆ High | 120–170g |
| Shrimp, scallops, crab | ★☆☆ Moderate | 120–170g |
| Cod, halibut, snapper | ★☆☆ Moderate | 120–170g |
| Mussels, clams, oysters | ★☆☆ Moderate | 120–170g |

> **Preference:** Wild-caught > farmed. Prioritize fatty fish for EPA/DHA. Aim for at least 3 fish meals per week.

### Vegetables (unlimited — 5+ servings/day)

| Food | Examples | Serving Size |
|------|----------|:------------:|
| Leafy greens | Spinach, kale, arugula, Swiss chard, collards, romaine | 1 cup raw / ½ cup cooked |
| Cruciferous | Broccoli, cauliflower, Brussels sprouts, cabbage | ½ cup cooked |
| Alliums | Onions, garlic, leeks, shallots, scallions | ½ onion / 1-2 cloves garlic |
| Peppers | Bell peppers (all colors), chili peppers, jalapeños | 1 medium |
| Squash/zucchini | Zucchini, yellow squash, spaghetti squash | ½ cup cooked |
| Nightshades | Tomatoes, eggplant, tomatillos | ½ cup cooked |
| Root vegetables | Carrots, beets, turnips, parsnips, radishes | ½ cup cooked |
| Cucumbers | All varieties | ½ cup sliced |
| Mushrooms | Cremini, portobello, shiitake, oyster | ½ cup cooked |
| Asparagus, green beans | Fresh or frozen | ½ cup cooked |
| Avocado | Technically a fruit; key fat source | ½ avocado |

> **Strict Paleo note:** Some versions restrict nightshades (tomatoes, peppers, eggplant) and starchy root vegetables. Standard Paleo allows all vegetables except white potatoes.

### Fruits (2–4 servings/day)

| Food | Examples | Serving Size |
|------|----------|:------------:|
| Berries | Blueberries, strawberries, raspberries, blackberries | 1 cup |
| Citrus | Oranges, lemons, limes, grapefruit | 1 medium |
| Stone fruit | Peaches, plums, apricots, cherries | 1 medium |
| Tropical | Bananas, mangoes, pineapple, papaya | ½ cup |
| Apples, pears | Fresh, with skin | 1 medium |
| Melons | Watermelon, cantaloupe, honeydew | 1 cup |
| Grapes, figs | Fresh only | 1 cup / 3-4 figs |

> **Weight loss note:** If weight loss is a goal, moderate intake of high-sugar fruits (bananas, mangoes, grapes, dried fruit) and prioritize berries and lower-sugar fruits.

### Nuts & Seeds (1–2 servings/day)

| Food | Serving Size |
|------|:------------:|
| Almonds, walnuts, macadamias | ~30g (small handful) |
| Pecans, Brazil nuts, pistachios | ~30g |
| Cashews | ~30g (technically Paleo; some strict versions exclude due to lectins) |
| Pumpkin seeds, sunflower seeds | ~30g |
| Flaxseeds, chia seeds | 1–2 tbsp |
| Coconut (fresh, shredded, or as flour/milk) | As desired |
| Nut butters (almond, cashew) — no added sugar/oil | 1–2 tbsp |

### Healthy Fats & Oils (liberal use)

| Food | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Extra virgin olive oil | Primary cooking and dressing oil |
| Avocado oil | High smoke point; good for high-heat cooking |
| Coconut oil | Stable for cooking; used liberally in Primal versions |
| Tallow, lard (pasture-raised) | Traditional cooking fats |
| Ghee (clarified butter) | Allowed in modified/Primal; excluded in strict |
| Duck fat, chicken schmaltz | Traditional fats |
| Avocado | Whole-food fat source |

### Eggs (1–3/day)

- Pasture-raised preferred (higher in omega-3 and vitamin D)
- All preparations acceptable
- Include in frittatas, scrambles, hard-boiled as snacks

### Beverages

| Beverage | Status |
|----------|--------|
| Water | Primary beverage — unlimited |
| Sparkling water | Allowed |
| Coffee | Allowed in most versions (excluded by purists); black or with coconut milk |
| Tea (green, herbal, black) | Allowed |
| Bone broth | Encouraged — nutrient-dense, gut-healing |
| Coconut water | Allowed (natural) |
| Alcohol | Excluded by purists; red wine accepted by many in moderation |

## Foods to Eat Frequently (Weekly)

### Sweet Potatoes & Starchy Tubers (3–5x/week)
- Sweet potatoes, yams, butternut squash, acorn squash
- Important carbohydrate source, especially for active individuals
- Cassava, plantains, taro — acceptable whole-food starches
- **Excluded in strict Cordain** (white potatoes always excluded)

### Coconut Products (as desired)
- Coconut milk (full-fat, canned), coconut flour, coconut aminos (soy sauce substitute), unsweetened shredded coconut

### Natural Sweeteners (sparingly)
- Honey (raw preferred)
- Maple syrup (pure)
- Coconut sugar
- **Note:** These are allowed but should be used sparingly. The Cordain original discourages all added sweeteners; most modern practitioners allow small amounts.

## Foods to Strictly Avoid (Eliminated)

| Food Category | Examples | Reason Excluded |
|---------------|----------|-----------------|
| **All grains** | Wheat, rice, oats, corn, barley, rye, millet, sorghum, teff | Post-agricultural; gluten, phytates, high glycemic load |
| **All products made from grains** | Bread, pasta, cereal, tortillas, crackers, flour | Same as above |
| **All legumes** | Beans (all types), lentils, chickpeas, peas, peanuts, soy, tofu, tempeh | Lectins, phytates; post-agricultural |
| **Dairy** | Milk, cheese, yogurt, butter, cream, whey, casein | Post-agricultural; lactose, casein protein |
| **Refined sugar** | White sugar, brown sugar, HFCS, agave | Processed; drives metabolic disease |
| **Refined/seed oils** | Canola, soybean, sunflower, safflower, corn, cottonseed, margarine | High omega-6; industrially processed |
| **White potatoes** | All forms | High glycemic load (excluded in strict; allowed in modified) |
| **Added salt** | Table salt, sea salt (in strict versions) | Not consumed pre-agriculture (Paleyolithic salt intake was very low) |
| **Processed foods** | Anything with artificial ingredients, preservatives, emulsifiers | Industrial era; numerous health concerns |
| **Soda, juice** | All sugary drinks | Refined sugar; processed |

## Foods to Limit / Minimize

| Food | Reason |
|------|--------|
| **Natural sweeteners** (honey, maple syrup, dates) | Still concentrated sugar; use sparingly for flavoring, not daily |
| **Dried fruit** | Concentrated sugar; easy to overconsume |
| **High-sugar fruits** (bananas, grapes, mangoes) | Moderate portions; prioritize berries for weight loss goals |
| **Nuts** | Healthy but calorie-dense; portion control needed for weight management |
| **Coffee** | Limit to 2-3 cups/day; purists exclude entirely |
| **Alcohol** | Exclude entirely (purist) or limit to occasional red wine (moderate practitioners) |
| **"Paleo" treats** | Paleo cookies, muffins, pancakes made with almond/coconut flour — still calorie-dense treats |

## Daily Eating Pattern (Sample Day)

### Breakfast
- 2-3 eggs scrambled with spinach, mushrooms, and bell peppers, cooked in olive oil or coconut oil
- ½ avocado sliced
- Fresh berries (blueberries or strawberries)
- Black coffee or herbal tea
- *Or:* Sweet potato hash with ground beef, onions, and eggs on top

### Lunch
- Large mixed greens salad with grilled chicken or salmon
- Cherry tomatoes, cucumber, red onion, olives, walnuts
- Dressing: olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, herbs
- *Or:* Leftover dinner (e.g., slow-cooked beef stew with root vegetables)

### Snack (optional)
- Handful of almonds or macadamia nuts
- Carrot and celery sticks with guacamole
- *Or:* Hard-boiled egg with salt-free seasoning

### Dinner
- Grilled wild salmon or grass-fed steak
- Roasted sweet potato wedges with coconut oil
- Steamed broccoli or roasted Brussels sprouts
- Side salad with olive oil dressing

### Dessert (optional)
- Fresh fruit (berries with coconut milk, or baked apple with cinnamon)
- *Or:* A few squares of dark chocolate (85%+ cacao, no soy lecithin — in moderate versions)

## Weekly Meal Structure (Sample)

| Day | Breakfast | Lunch | Dinner |
|-----|-----------|-------|--------|
| Monday | Eggs with avocado and berries | Chicken salad with olive oil dressing | Grilled salmon with roasted vegetables |
| Tuesday | Sweet potato hash with eggs | Leftover salmon with salad | Beef stir-fry with broccoli, peppers, coconut aminos |
| Wednesday | Berry smoothie (coconut milk, spinach) | Leftover beef stir-fry | Pork tenderloin with sweet potato mash and asparagus |
| Thursday | Eggs with spinach and mushrooms | Tuna salad (avocado-oil mayo) over greens | Baked cod with cauliflower rice and roasted carrots |
| Friday | Leftover frittata | Chicken and vegetable soup (bone broth base) | Grass-fed burgers (no bun) with sweet potato fries |
| Saturday | Omelet with mushrooms and onions | Sardines with mixed greens and olives | Slow-cooked lamb stew with root vegetables |
| Sunday | Pancakes (banana-egg, no flour) | Leftover lamb stew | Roast chicken with Brussels sprouts and squash |

> **Note:** The Paleo pattern typically features **protein at every meal** and **vegetables as the base of every plate**. Carbohydrate intake comes primarily from fruits and starchy vegetables rather than grains. Active individuals and athletes should include more starchy tubers (sweet potatoes, plantains) to meet energy needs.

## Key Nutritional Considerations

### Nutrients Adequately Provided
- **Protein:** High intake from meat, fish, eggs (typically 1.0–1.5 g/kg/day)
- **Healthy fats:** Monounsaturated (olive oil, avocado), omega-3 (fish), medium-chain (coconut)
- **Fiber:** From vegetables, fruits, nuts, seeds — adequate without grains
- **Micronutrients (generally high):** Vitamin B12, iron, zinc (from animal foods); vitamin C, folate, potassium, magnesium (from plants)
- **Antioxidants:** High from diverse fruits and vegetables

### Nutrients at Risk of Deficiency
| Nutrient | Risk Level | Reason | Solution |
|----------|:---------:|--------|----------|
| **Calcium** | **High** | No dairy; plant calcium is less bioavailable | Include sardines with bones, bone broth, broccoli, kale, almonds, fortified alternatives |
| **Vitamin D** | Moderate | If limited fatty fish or sun exposure | Fatty fish, egg yolks, sun exposure, supplement |
| **Iodine** | Moderate | No iodized salt (strict versions); limited seafood | Include seaweed, fish, shellfish regularly |
| **Folate** | Moderate | No fortified grains; adequate from vegetables if variety is high | Leafy greens, liver, beets |
| **Thiamine (B1)** | Low-Moderate | No fortified grains | Pork, sunflower seeds, macadamia nuts |
| **Magnesium** | Low | Generally adequate from nuts/seeds but monitor | Pumpkin seeds, almonds, spinach, dark chocolate |
| **Carbohydrate** (for athletes) | Moderate | Low-carb may impair high-intensity performance | Include sweet potatoes, plantains, fruits; consider modified Paleo |
