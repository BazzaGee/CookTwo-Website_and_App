# Low FODMAP Diet — Food Framework

> Practical guide to what to eat, avoid, and limit on the Low FODMAP diet across all three phases, including macronutrient targets, FODMAP subgroup categorization, and sample meal structures.

---

## Macronutrient Targets

The Low FODMAP diet does **not** prescribe specific macronutrient ratios — it is a **carbohydrate-type** restriction, not a macronutrient restriction. Total carbohydrate, protein, and fat intakes should remain similar to the individual's pre-diet baseline.

| Macronutrient | Target | Notes |
|---------------|--------|-------|
| **Carbohydrates** | ~45–55% of calories (typical) | Same as baseline, but *types* of carbs change |
| **Protein** | ~15–20% of calories | Unchanged — most protein sources are low FODMAP |
| **Fat** | ~25–35% of calories | Unchanged — fats are not FODMAPs |
| **FODMAP intake** | <5g/day (elimination phase) | Monash threshold for strict elimination |
| **Fiber** | 20–30g/day (monitor) | Fiber sources change; ensure adequacy |
| **Calcium** | 1,000–1,300 mg/day | Critical to maintain via lactose-free dairy or alternatives |

> The Low FODMAP diet is **not** a low-carb diet. It reduces specific fermentable carbohydrates, not carbohydrates overall. Rice, oats, quinoa, potatoes, and many fruits are all permitted in appropriate portions.

## FODMAP Subgroup Quick Reference

Understanding the five FODMAP subgroups is essential for the reintroduction phase:

| Subgroup | Abbreviation | Key High-FODMAP Foods | Low-FODMAP Swap |
|----------|-------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **Fructans** | FOS | Wheat, rye, onion, garlic, asparagus | Rice, oats, quinoa, spring onion greens, garlic-infused oil |
| **Galacto-oligosaccharides** | GOS | Lentils, chickpeas, beans, soy milk | Tofu, tempeh, canned lentils (small portions) |
| **Lactose** | — | Cow's milk, yogurt, soft cheese, ice cream | Lactose-free milk, hard cheeses, lactose-free yogurt |
| **Excess Fructose** | — | Apples, pears, honey, agave, mango, watermelon | Bananas, blueberries, oranges, kiwi, maple syrup |
| **Sorbitol (polyol)** | — | Avocado (large), apples, pears, blackberries, mushrooms | Avocado (small portion), strawberries, bell peppers |
| **Mannitol (polyol)** | — | Mushrooms, cauliflower, celery | Zucchini, spinach, green beans |

## Foods to Eat Daily (Low FODMAP — Elimination Phase)

### Grains & Starches (3–6 servings/day)

| Food | Examples | Serving Size |
|------|----------|:------------:|
| Rice | White, brown, basmati, jasmine, arborio | 1 cup cooked |
| Oats | Rolled, quick oats (not large portions) | ½ cup cooked |
| Quinoa | All types | 1 cup cooked |
| Gluten-free grains | Millet, sorghum, teff, buckwheat | ½ cup cooked |
| Potatoes | White, sweet (moderate portions) | 1 medium |
| Corn | Cornmeal/polenta, corn tortillas, popcorn | 1 cup / 2 tortillas |
| Rice noodles | All types | 1 cup cooked |
| Sourdough (spelt or wheat, slow-fermented) | Authentic long-fermented sourdough | 1–2 slices |
| GF bread/crackers | Check labels for hidden FODMAPs | 1–2 slices |

> **Note on wheat:** Small amounts of wheat in products like soy sauce (1–2 tbsp) are generally well-tolerated due to the small total fructan load per serving.

### Proteins (2–3 servings/day)

| Food | Examples | Serving Size |
|------|----------|:------------:|
| Chicken & turkey | All preparations | 100–150g |
| Fish | Salmon, cod, tuna, trout, all fish | 100–150g |
| Seafood | Shrimp, scallops, oysters, mussels | 100–150g |
| Eggs | All preparations | 1–2 large |
| Tofu (firm) | All firm/extra-firm tofu | 150g |
| Tempeh | Plain, unflavored | 100g |
| Beef & pork | Lean cuts | 100–150g |
| Lamb | Lean cuts | 100–150g |
| Hard cheeses | Cheddar, Swiss, parmesan, brie, camembert | 40g |
| Lactose-free dairy | Lactose-free milk, yogurt, cottage cheese | 1 cup |

> **Note:** Plain meat, fish, poultry, and eggs contain **zero FODMAPs**. The concern is with marinades, sauces, and processed forms that may contain onion, garlic, or honey.

### Fats & Oils (liberal)

| Food | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Olive oil | Safe — all types |
| Coconut oil | Safe |
| Butter | Safe — not a FODMAP |
| Peanut butter (plain) | 2 tbsp max |
| Garlic-infused olive oil | **Safe** — FODMAPs are water-soluble, not oil-soluble. This is a key flavor hack. |
| Herb-infused oils | Safe |

> **Garlic-infused oil is a critical tool** for the low-FODMAP diet. FODMAPs (fructans) in garlic are water-soluble but **not** fat-soluble, meaning garlic steeped in oil infuses flavor without FODMAPs. This allows onion/garlic flavor in cooking.

## Foods to Avoid (High FODMAP — Elimination Phase)

### High-Fructan Foods
| Food | FODMAP Type |
|------|-------------|
| Wheat (large amounts) — bread, pasta, cereal | Fructan |
| Rye — bread, crackers | Fructan |
| Barley | Fructan |
| Onions — all types (red, white, spring onion white, shallots) | Fructan |
| Garlic — all forms (fresh, powder, salt) | Fructan |
| Leeks (bulb) | Fructan |
| Asparagus | Fructan |
| Artichokes (globe) | Fructan |
| Chicory root / inulin | Fructan |
| Beets (large amounts) | Fructan |
| Brussels sprouts | Fructan |
| Savoy cabbage | Fructan |

### High-GOS Foods
| Food | FODMAP Type |
|------|-------------|
| Lentils (large amounts, especially red) | GOS |
| Chickpeas (large amounts) | GOS |
| Kidney beans, black beans, navy beans | GOS |
| Soy milk (made from whole soybeans) | GOS + fructan |
| Peas (large amounts) | GOS |
| Pistachios | GOS/fructan |
| Cashews | GOS/fructan |

### High-Lactose Foods
| Food | FODMAP Type |
|------|-------------|
| Cow's milk | Lactose |
| Regular yogurt | Lactose |
| Ice cream | Lactose |
| Ricotta cheese | Lactose |
| Cottage cheese (regular) | Lactose |
| Soft cheeses (mascarpone) | Lactose |
| Cream cheese (large amounts) | Lactose |
| Evaporated / condensed milk | Lactose |

### High-Fructose Foods
| Food | FODMAP Type |
|------|-------------|
| Apples | Excess fructose + sorbitol |
| Pears | Excess fructose + sorbitol |
| Watermelon | Excess fructose |
| Mangoes | Excess fructose |
| Honey | Excess fructose |
| Agave nectar | Excess fructose |
| High-fructose corn syrup | Excess fructose |
| Dried fruits (raisins, dates, figs, prunes) | Excess fructose |
| Cherries (large amounts) | Excess fructose + sorbitol |
| Fruit juices (apple, pear, mango) | Excess fructose |

### High-Polyol Foods
| Food | FODMAP Type |
|------|-------------|
| Avocado (large portions >¼) | Sorbitol |

## Sample Daily Meal Plan (Elimination Phase)

### Breakfast
- Gluten-free oats (½ cup) cooked with lactose-free milk
- Sliced banana and blueberries
- 10 almonds, chopped
- Sprinkle of cinnamon
- *Or:* 2 scrambled eggs with spinach and tomato, gluten-free toast with butter

### Lunch
- Grilled chicken breast (120g) over mixed greens (spinach, lettuce)
- Cherry tomatoes, cucumber, bell peppers
- ½ cup quinoa
- Dressing: olive oil + lemon juice + salt + pepper + herbs
- *Or:* Rice paper rolls with shrimp, lettuce, cucumber, carrot; dipping sauce (soy sauce + lime)

### Snack
- Lactose-free yogurt (150g) with strawberries
- *Or:* Rice cakes with peanut butter (2 tbsp) + banana

### Dinner
- Baked salmon (150g) with lemon and dill
- Roasted potatoes with rosemary and olive oil
- Steamed green beans and zucchini
- *Or:* Firm tofu stir-fry with bell pepper, bok choy, and carrots in garlic-infused oil + soy sauce, over rice

### Dessert (optional)
- 2 squares dark chocolate (70%+)
- *Or:* Fresh kiwi and mandarin

## The Reintroduction Phase Schedule (Sample)

A typical 7-subgroup testing protocol spread over 6–8 weeks:

| Week | FODMAP Subgroup Tested | Test Food (Day 1) | Test Food (Day 2) | Test Food (Day 3) | Washout |
|------|------------------------|-------------------|-------------------|-------------------|---------|
| 1 | Fructans (wheat) | 1 slice wheat bread | 2 slices wheat bread | 3 slices wheat bread | 3–4 days |
| 2 | Lactose | ½ cup cow's milk | 1 cup cow's milk | 1 cup + ice cream | 3–4 days |
| 3 | Excess fructose | 1 tbsp honey | 2 tbsp honey | 3 tbsp honey / ½ mango | 3–4 days |
| 4 | Fructans (onion/garlic) | ¼ onion / 1 clove garlic | ½ onion / 2 cloves | 1 onion / 3 cloves | 3–4 days |
| 5 | GOS | ¼ cup chickpeas | ½ cup chickpeas | ½ cup lentils | 3–4 days |
| 6 | Sorbitol | ¼ avocado | ½ avocado | 1 avocado | 3–4 days |
| 7 | Mannitol | 3 mushrooms | 5 mushrooms | ½ cup mushrooms | 3–4 days |

> For each test, symptoms are rated on a 0–10 scale. If symptoms occur, stop testing that subgroup, return to low-FODMAP, wash out for 3–4 days, then move to the next subgroup.

## Key Nutritional Considerations

### Nutrients Adequately Provided
- **Protein:** Abundant from meat, fish, poultry, eggs, tofu, hard cheese
- **Healthy fats:** Olive oil, butter, nuts (in limited quantities)
- **Vitamins & minerals:** Adequate from permitted vegetables, fruits, and protein sources
- **Carbohydrates:** Adequate from rice, potatoes, oats, quinoa, permitted fruits

### Nutrients to Monitor
| Nutrient | Risk Level | Concern | Solution |
|----------|:---------:|---------|----------|
| **Calcium** | High | If lactose-containing dairy eliminated without substitution | Lactose-free milk/yogurt, hard cheese, calcium-fortified plant milk |
| **Fiber** | Moderate | Less fiber from eliminated grains/legumes | Increase low-FODMAP fiber: oats, kiwi, chia, potato skins |
| **Iron** | Low-Moderate | If meat intake is reduced | Adequate from meat, poultry, fish; pair with vitamin C |
| **B vitamins** | Low | If wheat eliminated (fortified cereals) | GF fortified grains, eggs, meat |
| **Vitamin D** | Low | If dairy reduced | Fortified lactose-free milk, fatty fish, sunlight |
