# McDougall Diet / Starch Solution — Food Framework

> Practical guide to what to eat, avoid, and limit on the McDougall/Starch Solution diet, including macronutrient targets, portion guidelines, and sample meal structures.

---

## Macronutrient Targets

| Macronutrient | Target (% of total calories) | Notes |
|---------------|:----------------------------:|-------|
| **Carbohydrates** | 70–80% | Primarily from whole-food starches (potatoes, rice, beans, corn, grains) |
| **Protein** | 12–15% | Adequate but not high; all from plant sources (beans, grains, vegetables) |
| **Fat** | 5–15% | As low as practically achievable; **zero added fats**; natural fat only from whole plants |
| **Saturated fat** | <3% | Near-zero from whole plant foods |
| **Fiber** | 35–60 g/day | Very high — from whole grains, beans, vegetables |
| **Sodium** | <2,000 mg/day (target) | No added salt in the strict program; soy sauce limited |

> The McDougall Diet is a **high-carbohydrate, very low-fat** diet. Unlike most modern dietary recommendations that moderate all three macronutrients, McDougall explicitly maximizes carbohydrate (from starch) and minimizes fat to the lowest practical level. There is **no calorie limit** — adherents are encouraged to eat unlimited quantities of approved foods until satisfied.

## Foods to Eat Daily (The Base)

### Starches — The Foundation of Every Meal (Unlimited)

Starches should make up the **largest portion** of every meal — typically 50–80% of the plate by volume.

| Food | Examples | Serving Size |
|------|----------|:------------:|
| **Potatoes** | Russet, Yukon gold, red, fingerling — baked, boiled, steamed, mashed (no oil) | Unlimited |
| **Sweet potatoes & yams** | All varieties — baked, steamed, microwaved | Unlimited |
| **Rice** | Brown, white, basmati, jasmine, arborio, wild rice | Unlimited |
| **Beans & legumes** | Black beans, pinto, kidney, navy, cannellini, garbanzo (chickpeas), adzuki, edamame | Unlimited |
| **Lentils** | Brown, green, red, French (du Puy) | Unlimited |
| **Peas** | Green peas, split peas (yellow/green), black-eyed peas | Unlimited |
| **Corn** | Fresh, frozen, or on the cob | Unlimited |
| **Winter squash** | Butternut, acorn, kabocha, spaghetti squash, pumpkin | Unlimited |
| **Oats** | Steel-cut, rolled, or quick oats (not instant with additives) | Unlimited |
| **Barley** | Hulled or pearl | Unlimited |
| **Wheat** | Whole wheat berries, bulgur, couscous (whole wheat), farro | Unlimited |
| **Quinoa** | All colors (white, red, black, tri-color) | Unlimited |
| **Buckwheat** | Groats, soba noodles (100% buckwheat) | Unlimited |
| **Millet, teff, sorghum** | Less common but fully approved grains | Unlimited |
| **Whole grain pasta** | Whole wheat, brown rice, or bean pasta (no egg) | Unlimited |
| **Bread** | Whole grain, sourdough (no oil or dairy) | Unlimited (maintenance); limited (MWL) |

> **McDougall's principle:** "Eat until you are satisfied. There is no need to count calories or control portions. Your body will naturally regulate intake on a diet of whole starches and vegetables."

### Vegetables (Generous — with Every Meal)

| Food | Examples | Serving Size |
|------|----------|:------------:|
| **Leafy greens** | Spinach, kale, collards, chard, romaine, arugula | Unlimited |
| **Cruciferous** | Broccoli, cauliflower, Brussels sprouts, cabbage, bok choy | Unlimited |
| **Root vegetables** | Carrots, beets, turnips, parsnips, radishes | Unlimited |
| **Alliums** | Onions, garlic, leeks, shallots, scallions | Unlimited |
| **Squash (summer)** | Zucchini, yellow squash, pattypan | Unlimited |
| **Nightshades** | Tomatoes, bell peppers, eggplant (McDougall allows all nightshades) | Unlimited |
| **Green beans** | Snap beans, string beans, wax beans | Unlimited |
| **Mushrooms** | All varieties | Unlimited |
| **Celery, cucumber, asparagus** | As side dishes and in salads | Unlimited |

> **Note:** Vegetables are encouraged generously but are **secondary to starches**. McDougall emphasizes that vegetables alone do not provide enough calories for sustainable energy — that is why starches are the center of the diet.

### Fruits (Moderate — 1–3 servings/day)

| Food | Serving Size |
|------|:------------:|
| Fresh fruit (apples, bananas, oranges, berries, melon, etc.) | 1–3 servings/day |
| Dried fruit (raisins, dates, figs) | Occasional, small amounts (high sugar density) |

> **Weight loss note:** For those actively pursuing weight loss (Maximum Weight Loss version), McDougall recommends limiting fruit to 1–2 servings/day due to fructose content. At maintenance, fruit can be eaten more freely.

## Foods to Eat Occasional / Limited

### Nuts, Seeds, and High-Fat Plant Foods (Minimal)

| Food | Recommendation |
|------|---------------|
| **Flaxseed** (ground) | 1 tbsp/day — recommended for omega-3 (ALA) content |
| **Walnuts** | Occasional small amounts (a few walnuts) — only at ideal body weight |
| **Other nuts** (almonds, cashews, pistachios) | Rarely, small amounts — only at ideal body weight |
| **Seeds** (chia, hemp, pumpkin, sunflower) | Occasional, small amounts |
| **Avocado** | Small amounts only at ideal body weight; avoid during weight loss |
| **Olives** | Avoid (high fat) or very rare small amounts |
| **Coconut** | Avoid (high saturated fat) |

> McDougall argues that high-fat plant foods are "optional and minimal" — they are not necessary for health, and they add unnecessary calories that can stall weight loss. The exception is flaxseed, which he recommends for its omega-3 content.

### Soy Products (Limited)

| Food | Recommendation |
|------|---------------|
| **Tofu, tempeh** | Occasional — McDougall considers these acceptable but higher in fat and protein than ideal |
| **Edamame** | OK (whole soybean, lower in fat than processed soy) |
| **Soy milk** | OK in moderation (unsweetened) |
| **Soy sauce / tamari** | OK for flavoring (use low-sodium; counts toward sodium) |

## Foods to STRICTLY AVOID

### All Animal Products

| Food | Reason |
|------|--------|
| **Meat** (beef, pork, lamb, poultry) | Saturated fat, cholesterol, inflammatory compounds |
| **Fish & seafood** | Cholesterol, saturated fat; McDougall does not recommend fish |
| **Eggs** | Highest cholesterol food; 200+ mg per egg |
| **Dairy** (milk, cheese, yogurt, butter, cream) | Saturated fat, cholesterol; McDougall considers dairy a major health hazard |
| **Gelatin, collagen** | Animal-derived |

### All Added Fats and Oils

| Food | Reason |
|------|--------|
| **All vegetable oils** (olive, canola, coconut, sunflower, safflower, sesame, avocado, etc.) | 100% fat; 120 calories per tablespoon; McDougall considers all extracted oils harmful |
| **Butter & ghee** | Animal fat + saturated fat |
| **Margarine** | Processed fat |
| **Shortening & lard** | Pure fat |
| **Oil-based dressings & sauces** | Hidden fat |

> **McDougall on olive oil:** Despite its reputation as "heart-healthy," McDougall argues that olive oil is 100% fat with negligible nutrients, that it impairs endothelial function (citing Vogel 2000), and that populations eating olive oil (Mediterranean) are healthy *in spite of* olive oil, not because of it. This view is controversial and contradicted by PREDIMED evidence.

### Refined and Processed Foods

| Food | Reason |
|------|--------|
| **White flour products** (white bread, regular pasta, crackers) | Stripped of fiber and nutrients; rapid glycemic impact |
| **Refined sugar** | Empty calories; McDougall allows minimal natural sweetness (e.g., a little maple syrup or date sugar) |
| **Sugary drinks** (soda, juice, sports drinks) | Empty calories |
| **Ultra-processed foods** | Chemical additives; unknown health effects |
| **Artificial sweeteners** | Potential metabolic disruption; not whole foods |
| **Vegan junk food** (vegan donuts, vegan cheese, vegan burgers with oil) | May be vegan but violates the whole-food, no-oil principle |

### Beverages to Limit or Avoid

| Beverage | Recommendation |
|----------|---------------|
| **Water** | Primary beverage — unlimited |
| **Tea** (herbal or green) | OK, unsweetened |
| **Coffee** | Discouraged (McDougall argues against caffeine) but not strictly banned |
| **Alcohol** | Discouraged — empty calories; may impair judgment about food choices |
| **Fruit juice** | Limit — high sugar without fiber |
| **Soda** | Avoid |

## Approved Flavorings (How to Make Starch Taste Good Without Oil)

The most common challenge on the McDougall diet is making food flavorful without oil, butter, or dairy. McDougall teaches extensive oil-free cooking techniques:

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Herbs** (dried or fresh) | Basil, oregano, thyme, rosemary, sage, parsley, cilantro, dill, bay leaf |
| **Spices** | Cumin, coriander, turmeric, paprika, cinnamon, nutmeg, cardamom, ginger, garlic powder, onion powder, chili powder, cayenne |
| **Acids** | Lemon juice, lime juice, vinegars (balsamic, apple cider, rice, red wine) |
| **Umami** | Nutritional yeast (use liberally for cheesy flavor), miso paste (small amounts), soy sauce / tamari / liquid aminos |
| **Salty/savory** | Low-sodium soy sauce, salt-free seasoning blends, mustard (no oil), hot sauce (no oil) |
| **Sweet** | Small amounts of date sugar, maple syrup, or fruit-based sweeteners |
| **Sautéing without oil** | Use water, vegetable broth, or wine for sautéing onions, garlic, and vegetables |
| **Salad dressing** | Lemon or lime juice + mustard + herbs; blended tofu dressing (no oil); blended cashew dressing (maintenance only) |

## Daily Eating Pattern (Sample Day)

### Breakfast
- Large bowl of oatmeal (steel-cut or rolled) with:
  - Cinnamon and berries or sliced banana
  - A splash of plant milk (unsweetened soy or oat milk)
- *Or:* Hash brown potatoes (no oil) with sautéed onions, peppers, and spinach (water-sautéed)
- *Or:* Rice porridge (congee) with scallions and soy sauce
- Beverage: Herbal tea or water

### Lunch
- Large portion of rice or quinoa (2–3 cups cooked)
- Black bean or lentil stew or chili (oil-free)
- Steamed or roasted vegetables (broccoli, carrots, zucchini)
- Side salad with oil-free dressing (lemon-mustard-herb)
- *Or:* Bean burrito bowl: rice, beans, corn salsa, pico de gallo, lettuce (no cheese, no sour cream, no oil)

### Snack (optional)
- Baked potato or sweet potato (plain or with salt-free seasoning)
- Fresh fruit
- *Or:* Rice cakes with mashed avocado (maintenance only)

### Dinner
- Large plate of pasta (whole wheat) with oil-free marinara sauce and vegetables
- Large serving of steamed broccoli or green beans
- *Or:* Big bowl of hearty vegetable-bean soup with whole grain bread (no oil or butter)
- *Or:* Mashed potatoes (no butter or milk — use plant milk and nutritional yeast) with mushroom gravy (oil-free) and vegetables

### Dessert (optional)
- Fresh fruit
- *Or:* Baked apple with cinnamon and raisins

## Weekly Meal Structure (McDougall-Style)

| Day | Breakfast | Lunch | Dinner |
|-----|-----------|-------|--------|
| Monday | Oatmeal with berries & cinnamon | Rice with black bean chili & salad | Pasta with marinara & steamed broccoli |
| Tuesday | Potato hash with onions & peppers | Lentil soup with whole grain bread & side salad | Baked sweet potato with chickpea curry & spinach |
| Wednesday | Oatmeal with banana & walnuts (1 tbsp) | Quinoa bowl with kidney beans, corn & salsa | Miso soup with udon noodles & bok choy |
| Thursday | Breakfast rice porridge with soy sauce & scallions | Bean burrito bowl (rice, beans, corn, lettuce, salsa) | Hearty vegetable stew with barley & root vegetables |
| Friday | Oatmeal with apples & cinnamon | Split pea soup with rye bread & salad | Baked potato with oil-free mushroom gravy & green beans |
| Saturday | Pancakes (oil-free, whole grain) with maple syrup | Rice noodle stir-fry with tofu & vegetables (no oil) | Minestrone soup with whole grain bread |
| Sunday | Large breakfast hash (potatoes, beans, salsa, spinach) | Leftover soup or stew with rice | Bean and corn tacos with lettuce, tomato & hot sauce |

> **Note:** McDougall meals are intentionally simple and repetitive. The emphasis is on large portions of starch with vegetables. Herbs, spices, and sauces provide variety. Meals should be satisfying and filling — the goal is to never feel hungry.

## The McDougall Diet Progression Phases

McDougall teaches a phased approach for new adherents:

### Phase 1: Transition (Weeks 1–2)
- Replace one meal per day with a McDougall meal (usually breakfast: oatmeal)
- Gradually increase to 2 meals, then all meals
- Expect some digestive adjustment (increased fiber → gas, bloating) that resolves in 1–2 weeks
- Begin B12 supplementation immediately

### Phase 2: Full McDougall Diet (Weeks 3–4)
- All meals follow the Starch Solution
- No animal products, no added oils
- Eat unlimited approved foods
- Monitor: weight, blood pressure, blood sugar (if diabetic) — dramatic improvements expected

### Phase 3: Maximum Weight Loss (optional, for those needing to lose weight)
- Eliminate flour products (no bread, no pasta)
- Limit fruit to 1–2 servings/day
- No nuts, seeds, avocado
- Focus on whole, unprocessed starches and vegetables
- Continue until ideal body weight achieved

### Phase 4: Maintenance
- At ideal body weight: reintroduce whole grain bread, pasta, and moderate fruit
- Small amounts of nuts and avocado acceptable
- Still: no animal products, no added oils

## Key Nutritional Considerations

### Nutrients Adequately Provided
- **Carbohydrates (energy):** Abundantly supplied from starches
- **Fiber:** Among the highest-fiber diets possible (35–60 g/day)
- **Potassium:** Very high from potatoes, beans, vegetables
- **Magnesium:** From whole grains, beans, greens
- **Folate:** From beans, lentils, leafy greens
- **Vitamin C:** From potatoes, bell peppers, broccoli, fruit
- **Antioxidants/polyphenols:** From vegetables, whole grains, spices
- **Protein:** Adequate — ~50–60 g/day on 2,000 calories (12–15% of calories)

### Nutrients Requiring Supplementation or Monitoring

| Nutrient | Risk | Solution |
|----------|:----:|----------|
| **Vitamin B12** | **Critical — guaranteed deficiency without supplement** | Sublingual B12: 1,000 mcg 2–3x/week, or daily B12-fortified foods |
| **Vitamin D** | Moderate | Sunlight; 1,000–2,000 IU/day supplement; fortified plant milk |
| **Iodine** | Low-Moderate | Use iodized salt (in small amounts); or kelp supplement (careful with dosing) |
| **Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)** | Low-Moderate | 1 tbsp ground flaxseed daily (ALA); consider algae-based DHA supplement |
| **Calcium** | Low-Moderate | Fortified plant milk; calcium-set tofu; leafy greens; tahini |
| **Iron** | Low | Beans, lentils, fortified grains; pair with vitamin C for absorption |
| **Zinc** | Low-Moderate | Beans, whole grains, pumpkin seeds (maintenance) |
