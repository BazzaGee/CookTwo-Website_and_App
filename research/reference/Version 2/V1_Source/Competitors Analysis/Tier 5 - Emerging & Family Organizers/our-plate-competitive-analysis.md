## 1. Executive Summary

OurPlate is a couples-focused meal planning application currently in early access. It targets the specific pain point of dinner decision-making in relationships through a fairness-based approach (rating + turn-taking). The product claims 500+ couples and a 4.8/5 rating, though both figures are unverified and likely aspirational. The site is built on Lovable.dev (an AI website builder), which signals pre-seed/indie status. Privacy and Terms pages return 404 errors, indicating incomplete legal infrastructure. OurPlate occupies a narrow niche (couple dinner decisions) without the broader food system capabilities that Cupla delivers. Threat level is LOW-MEDIUM: the positioning is adjacent but the execution depth is limited.

## 7. Core Mechanism

**Primary Mechanism:** Fairness-as-a-Service

OurPlate's core differentiator is the fairness system:
1. Both partners independently rate meal options
2. The system applies a turn-taking algorithm
3. The "chooser" selects from mutually-acceptable options
4. Shopping list consolidates the week's selections

**Mechanism Strength:** Directly addresses a real relationship pain point (dinner arguments)

**Mechanism Weakness:** Reduces the meal decision to a binary fairness model. Real couples negotiate based on energy, time, cravings, what's expiring, what's on sale, etc. A rigid turn-taking system may feel mechanical.

**Comparison to Cupla:** Cupla's pantry-first AI approach is more sophisticated — it considers inventory, expiry, nutrition, and both partners' preferences simultaneously, producing suggestions that are contextually relevant rather than merely "fair."

1. **Monitor monthly:** Check ourplate.app for product updates, app store listings, or funding announcements
2. **Note the fairness mechanism:** The turn-taking concept could inform Cupla's relationship-first UX features — consider adding an optional "fair pick" mode
3. **Do not engage publicly:** OurPlate has no user base to poach and no market presence worth counter-positioning against

### Medium-Term Considerations
4. **Feature parity sprint:** If OurPlate gains traction, Cupla can replicate their rating + turn-taking features in a single sprint — the mechanism is trivially implementable
5. **SEO capture:** Cupla should rank for "couple meal planning," "stop arguing about dinner," and related terms — OurPlate's SEO is non-existent, making this easy territory

### Long-Term Positioning
6. **Differentiation message:** Cupla's pantry-first AI approach is fundamentally different from OurPlate's fairness model — emphasize "smart food management" over "fair decision making"
7. **Relationship-first without gimmicks:** Cupla can offer relationship-centric features (shared calendar, collaborative cooking) without reducing the experience to a turn-taking algorithm
